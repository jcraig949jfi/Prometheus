2. **State vector** \(x\in\{0,1,?\}^n\) holds truth assignments (0 =false, 1 =true, ? =unknown). Background knowledge fixes a subset \(x_B\).  
3. **Cost function** \(c(x)=\sum_{(i,j,r)\in E} w_r\cdot\phi_r(x_i,x_j)\) where \(\phi_r\) is 0 if the relation holds under \(x\) and 1 otherwise; \(w_r\) are fixed weights (e.g., \(w_{\text{implies}}=1.0\)). This is the instantaneous cost of violating a logical constraint.  
4. **Expected free energy** for a candidate trajectory \(\tau=\{x^{(0)},x^{(1)},…,x^{(T)}\}\) (where \(x^{(0)}\) is the parsed answer and each step applies a single edit – flip a literal, swap arguments, or insert/delete a relation) is:  
   \[
   G(\tau)=\underbrace{\sum_{t=0}^{T} \mathbb{E}[c(x^{(t)})]}_{\text{expected cost}}+\underbrace{\sum_{t=0}^{T} \mathcal{H}[b^{(t)}]}_{\text{epistemic term}}
   \]  
   The expectation is taken over a uniform distribution of worlds generated by property‑based testing (see step 5). The entropy term \(\mathcal{H}\) is computed from the variance of truth values across worlds (numpy var).  
5. **Property‑based testing**: generate \(M\) random worlds \(w^{(m)}\) consistent with \(x_B\) by sampling truth values for unfixed propositions. For each world evaluate \(c(x^{(0)})\); collect failing worlds \(F=\{w^{(m)}|c>0\}\). Apply a shrinking loop: iteratively remove literals from the answer and re‑test; stop when no further removal reduces \(|F|\). Let \(s=|F_{\text{min}}|\) be the size of the minimal failing set.  
6. **Score**:  
   \[
   \text{score}= \exp\bigl(-\alpha\, G(\tau^*) - \beta\, s\bigr)
   \]  
   where \(\tau^*\) is the trajectory with minimal \(G\) found by a greedy descent (equivalent to an LQR‑style step‑wise cost reduction), and \(\alpha,\beta\) are scaling constants (set to 0.5). Higher score indicates lower expected free energy and larger robustness to perturbations.
