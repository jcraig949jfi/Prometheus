# Literature Search 3: Sleeping Beauties

## Top Sleeping Beauties (under-cited, directly relevant)
1. **Krstovski & Blei. "Equation Embeddings." arXiv:1803.09123 (2018).** Embeds equations in shared vector space using context. Closest prior work to our shared latent space. Modestly cited.
2. **Watanabe. "Algebraic Geometry and Statistical Learning Theory." Cambridge (2009).** Birational invariants govern neural net learning. Deep AG↔statistics bridge. Under-cited.
3. **He. "ML meets Number Theory: Data Science of BSD." arXiv:1911.02008 (2019).** TDA on 2.5M EC arithmetic invariants. Predecessor to murmurations. Our direct ancestor.
4. **Hu et al. "Personalized Tucker Decomposition." arXiv:2309.03439 (2023).** Shared-vs-local decomposition of heterogeneous tensor data. Directly relevant to our multi-domain tensor approach.
5. **Di Giovanni et al. "Heterogeneous manifolds for curvature-aware graph embedding." arXiv:2202.01185 (2022).** Local curvature variation in graph embeddings. Relevant to our ORC measurements.
6. **Dadure et al. "Semantic Representations of Math Expressions." arXiv:2211.08142 (2022).** Embedding mathematical equivalence classes. Analogy-solving on math identities.
7. **Osborne. "Alethfeld / Adversarial Prover." GitHub (2026).** Independent validation of Generator-Adversary methodology for math.
8. **Greiner-Petter et al. "Math-word embedding." Scientometrics 125(3) (2020).** Word2vec discovers math analogies on DLMF text.

## Arithmetic Statistics + ML (richest vein)
9. He, Lee, Oliver. "Machine learning invariants of arithmetic curves." J. Symbolic Comput. (2023). ML predicts rank/torsion from LMFDB.
10. Bober, Booker, Lee, Lowry-Duda. "Murmurations of modular forms." arXiv:2310.07746 (2025). Extends murmurations to MF.
11. He et al. "Murmurations: A Case Study in AI-Assisted Mathematics." arXiv:2603.09680 (2026). Retrospective on the paradigm.
12. He. "Unveiling Arithmetic Statistics via Data Science." arXiv:2509.03129 (2025). ML on 3M congruent number curves.

## Classical foundations
13. Bhargava. "On Class Numbers, Torsion, and Quadratic Twists of EC." arXiv:2007.08756 (2020). MAPS between torsion and class groups. Closest to our torsion-class number axis finding.
14. Conductor-Discriminant Formula (Hasse/Artin). Classical. They encode the same ramification data — but nobody framed them as one axis.
15. Vojta dictionary (Diophantine ↔ Nevanlinna). Height as cross-domain invariant.

## Recent AI + Math (2024-2026)
16. Georgiev, Gomez-Serrano, Tao, Wagner. "AlphaEvolve." arXiv:2511.02864 (2025). LLM-guided evolutionary math discovery.
17. He. "Mathematics: The Rise of the Machines." arXiv:2511.17203 (2025). Survey of ML on pure math.
18. Carbone. "Advancing mathematics research with generative AI." arXiv:2511.07420 (2025).

## Adversarial science

## Genuine novelty gaps (what we do that nobody else has done)
- Nobody framed conductor/discriminant/level as a single complexity axis
- Nobody stated torsion and class number collapse onto one dimension
- Nobody used phonemic/linguistic analogies for mathematical structure
- Nobody proposed height as a cross-domain universal invariant computationally
- The motivic program remains entirely non-computational
- Nobody embedded 38 domains in one shared space with verified cross-domain transfer
