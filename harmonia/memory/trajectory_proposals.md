# Harmonia Trajectory Proposals

**Purpose:** when James polls multiple Harmonia instances with the same
prompt — "after your work session, what do you think would most help
our trajectory?" — their responses are collected here. This is the
methodology applied to ourselves: rather than any single instance's
vision becoming the project's direction, the *distribution* of
proposals across instances is the first-order signal. Agreement across
independent Harmonias suggests robust direction; disagreement maps the
axes of strategic uncertainty that the conductor still needs to
decide.

**Meta-note on epistemic status.** This document is itself an instance
of `MULTI_PERSPECTIVE_ATTACK@v1` deployed on the question *"what
should Prometheus do next?"*. Each instance is a thread under
a disciplinary prior shaped by whatever work it was doing that
session. The synthesis-over-instances is the conductor's job, and
should not be performed prematurely — hold for ≥ 3 instances before
integrating.

**Format.** For each instance contribution:
1. Session context (what the instance was working on before proposing).
2. The proposals, verbatim.
3. What the instance bets against.
4. Refutable meta-prediction (if offered).
5. Stenographer commentary (Harmonia_M2_sessionA, the instance
   curating this doc; short takes — agreements, disagreements,
   overlaps with existing substrate artifacts).

## Instance 1 (2026-04-21, anonymized)

### Session context

Unknown at time of capture. Proposals reference concrete Prometheus

semantics, 10-generator roster, MNAR concerns, Lehmer constant as

composing the proposal.

### Proposal 1 — Mode-switch as a first-class invariance axis

> The tensor currently measures invariance across projections within
> one mathematical language (number theory + statistics). Add a
> second axis: invariance across mathematical languages. Every live
> specimen gets reframed in at least three distinct frames
> (functional-analytic, dynamical/ergodic, RMT/spectral, operator-
> theoretic, information-geometric, statistical-mechanical,
> categorical). A specimen that commits to structurally-equivalent
> predictions in three frames is real; a specimen that only exists
> in one frame is frame-bound.
>
> F011's rank-0 residual shouldn't just survive block-shuffle — it
> should commit to a mass-gap number, a spectral-rigidity deviation,
> and a free-energy excess, and those three commits should agree to
> spec. If they do, the specimen earns a tier above +2 — call it
> **language-invariant**. If one commit contradicts another, the
> specimen is frame-bound and the contradiction is itself the
> discovery.
>
> Capture guardrail: the three frames must have demonstrably
> different inductive biases. Three cosmetic rephrasings don't count.

### Proposal 2 — Every claim carries a Lehmer-style falsifiable number

> Right now tensor entries are qualitative (−2..+2) with effect-size
> metadata. Replace: every +1 and +2 cell carries an explicit
> numerical commit with a pre-registered falsification threshold.
> Not "F011 residual survives under null X." Rather: "F011 residual
> is 22.90 ± 0.78%, predicted by mechanism M, falsified if measured
> at n > 10⁷ differs by > 2σ with plausible instrument." The commit
> is the cell; the cell is the commit.
>
> This turns each cell into a Popperian object. The wave-2
> reviewer's critique that Harmonia is a "claim filter, not claim
> generator" dissolves — a pre-committed numerical claim IS
> generative, because it predicts values we haven't measured yet.
> The generating mode is the act of committing to the number before
> the data.
>
> Capture guardrail: refutation cadence. If no cell refutes in six
> months, we've added decimal-place decoration, not discipline.

### Proposal 3 — Open-problem atlas (problem-space dual)

> The charter says open problems are shortcut requests. Operationalize.
> Build a second tensor: rows = open problems (Lehmer, abc, BSD, RH,
> Goldbach, class number 1, Serre modularity, Langlands functoriality,
> Hodge, …); columns = mechanism-families (mass-gap, spectral
> rigidity, ergodic mixing, lattice compactness, cohomology
> vanishing, transfer-operator determinants, …). Entry = does this
> mechanism have anything committable to say about this problem?
>
> Zero = no. +1 = testable bridge. +2 = specific committed prediction
> (like my e^{1/(2π)} Lehmer stance).
>
> The rows with shared mechanism-profiles are secretly the same
> problem. Columns with broad row-support are secretly universal
> tools. A mechanism that commits against five problems unlocks five
> problems with one invention — this is what Langlands-shaped
> progress looks like operationally. The specimen atlas James
> proposed is the object-space dual. This is the problem-space dual.
> Both should run.
>
> Capture guardrail: at least 3 rows must share ≥ 2 mechanism-family
> columns with committed predictions before this tensor is treated
> as load-bearing. Otherwise it's a list, not a structure.

### Proposal 4 — Cross-frame sprints as the primary generating mode

> Shift the center of gravity. The current tensor-populate-verify-
> promote loop is the filter mode. The generating mode should be
> Lehmer-style sprints: weekly, pick one open problem, assign it
> to a Harmonia session, require a 30-minute commit in a non-native
> mathematical frame under (A)/(B)/(C) stance discipline with a
> refutable prediction before the session ends. No survey. No
> balanced review. No hedge. The sprint is the discipline.
>
> Sprint outputs populate the open-problem atlas (#3). Over 50
> sprints that's 50 bold commits across 50-ish problems, most of
> which will be wrong — but the wrongness profile itself is a new
> signal. 20th-century physics progressed this way: commit, be
> falsified hard, learn why. The formalism came later.
>
> Capture guardrail: every sprint output must include a numerical
> commit with a falsification threshold. Hedged outputs don't count
> as sprints.

### Stenographer commentary (Harmonia_M2_sessionA)

**Proposal 1 — strong agreement with a naming concern.** This is
`SHADOWS_ON_WALL@v1` operationalized at the specimen level — forcing
every specimen to commit in ≥3 frames is exactly the lens-count-to-
tier discipline applied to individual findings rather than to
open-problem attacks. The guardrail (three frames must have different
inductive biases) is the forbidden-move mechanism that
`MULTI_PERSPECTIVE_ATTACK@v1` already enforces at the problem level.
My concern: "language-invariant" as a new tier name duplicates what
`SHADOWS_ON_WALL` already calls `coordinate_invariant` / `durable`.
If we land this, use the existing tier vocabulary to avoid parallel
naming. Real addition is the *frame-bound* concept: a specimen whose
predictions contradict across frames is a diagnostic, not a failure —
the contradiction maps the frame's inductive-bias boundary. That's
worth its own pattern.

**Proposal 2 — strong agreement, already partially done, needs
universalization.** The wave-2 reviewer's "claim filter not claim
generator" critique is real, and this move is the right answer.
F011's 22.90 ± 0.78% IS already a Popperian commit; Track D is the
falsification test. The gap is universality — not every +1/+2 cell
has this structure. A `SIGNATURE@v3` schema extension would
structurally encode a numerical commit + falsification threshold +
refutation cadence on every cell. The six-month refutation-cadence
guardrail is sharp; if no cell refutes in six months we know the
commits are too loose. Small caveat: calibration anchors (F001–F005,
F008, F009) are Level-4 identities and don't have falsifiable
commits in the same sense — they verify rather than predict. The
schema should have a "calibration" escape hatch.

**Proposal 3 — directional agreement, deploy incrementally.** This
is literally the tensorized extension of `PROBLEM_LENS_CATALOG@v1`
that I built today (three catalogs: lehmer, collatz, p-vs-np). The
catalogs ARE rows; the lenses ARE columns; entries already encode
APPLIED / PUBLIC_KNOWN / UNAPPLIED. The instance's 3-rows-×-2-columns
guardrail is exactly right for load-bearing — at current 3 catalogs
and ~20 distinct disciplinary lens classes, we're *almost* at the
threshold. My recommendation: build 3–5 more catalogs first
(RH, abc, BSD, Goldbach, Langlands functoriality as candidates),
*then* promote the aggregate to a second tensor with proper
visualization via Cartographer. Premature tensorization locks in
column choices that should be discovered, not decreed.

**Proposal 4 — strong agreement, complements rather than replaces.**
Weekly cross-frame sprints are the `MULTI_PERSPECTIVE_ATTACK@v1`
rhythm — but single-thread-per-problem rather than 5-threads-per-
problem. The trade is: multi-perspective attack gets 5 views of one
problem (depth); a weekly sprint sequence gets 1 view of 50 problems
(breadth). Both matter. The atlas-population argument (Proposal 3
via Proposal 4's output) is the strongest structural case. My push-
back is only on "primary generating mode" — this should *complement*
multi-perspective attack, not replace it. The filter-vs-generator
framing Instance 1 is dissolving is real; generators ARE primary.
But we lose signal by only running single-thread sprints; some
problems demand the 5-thread depth.

**Bets-against, sorted by my agreement:**

- *More generators* → strong agree. 10 is saturated until Map-Elites
  (gen_01) and Definition DAG land. Further generators without those
  are bureaucratic growth.
- *Bigger tensors* → agree with a refinement: *volumetric* expansion
  (more rows/cols of same class) worsens MNAR, yes. But *orthogonal*
  expansion (a second tensor as in Proposal 3, or new-projection
  classes that fundamentally add invariance dimensions) is different.
  The instance's wording is correct but could be misread.
- *More external reviews* → partial agree. Human reviews at one-per-
  quarter cadence are right; cross-model runs of committed prompts
  (as we did with the mass-gap thread across 5 models) are a new
  class of external review that's cheap and valuable and shouldn't
  be lumped in.

**Meta-prediction — well-formed, ambitious, worth betting on.** The
methodology-to-mechanism shift prediction is refutable in exactly
the Popperian way Proposal 2 advocates. Two months is aggressive but
plausible if Proposals 1+2 land alone (Proposals 3+4 have longer
ramp). I'd structure it: adopt Proposals 1+2 first (substrate work),
let Proposals 3+4 develop naturally as #1+#2 mature, and evaluate
wave-3 against the shift-to-mechanism prediction at the 2-month
mark.

**Overall posture:** this is one of the sharper strategic takes I've
seen generated. High overlap with the existing epistemic stack (SHADOWS_

## Methodology notes for future capture

- **Anonymize instances by default** unless the instance explicitly
  identifies itself (canonical session name). Post-session the
  instance's work-history is partially recoverable from the sync
  stream but not always unique.
- **Do not synthesize before n ≥ 3.** The first instance's
  framing anchors subsequent readings if they see it; synthesis
  prematurely collapses the distribution.
- **Record session context where recoverable** — what the instance
  worked on shapes its proposals. A generator-heavy session produces
  generator-heavy proposals; an epistemics-heavy session produces
  epistemics-heavy proposals. That correlation is informative about
  sampling bias in the proposals themselves.
- **Stenographer commentary is clearly labeled** and does not
  displace the instance's voice. My takes are one reading of the
  proposal, not consensus.
- **Consider running the same proposals across different LLM models**
  once text is canonicalized — the cross-model variance data we
  gathered for the Lehmer mass-gap thread applies here. A
  single-model multi-instance sample has correlated sampling bias;
  cross-model sampling disentangles model-specific from methodology-
  robust proposals.
