# Attempt — Hodge Conjecture (Calabi-Yau 3-fold sub-case)

**Researcher:** Charon 3
**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Time spent:** ~40 min (literature scan + sub-case analysis + small computation)
**Verdict:** PARTIAL_RESULT (verified for quintic 3-fold trivially; obstruction documented for Schoen-type CY3)

## Problem statement

**Hodge conjecture (general, projective form):** Let X be a smooth complex projective variety. Every Hodge class in H^{2p}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X) is a ℚ-linear combination of cohomology classes of codimension-p algebraic subvarieties of X.

**Sub-case attacked:** X a smooth projective Calabi-Yau 3-fold (h^{0,0} = h^{3,0} = 1, h^{1,0} = h^{2,0} = 0, K_X = 0, simply connected). The non-trivial codimension is p = 2 (codim 1 is Lefschetz (1,1)-theorem, codim 3 is multiples of the fundamental class). So the question reduces to: **is every Hodge class in H⁴(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{2,2}(X) a ℚ-linear combination of classes of algebraic curves?**

By hard Lefschetz with the polarization, this is equivalent to whether every Hodge class in H²(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{1,1}(X) is algebraic — which IS Lefschetz (1,1). So actually the codim-2 problem on a CY3 *does* reduce to Lefschetz (1,1) **provided** the multiplication map H^{1,1} ⊗ H^{1,1} → H^{2,2} is surjective. **It is not, in general.** This is the heart of the matter.

## Literature scan: prior attempts

1. **Lefschetz 1924** ("L'Analysis Situs et la Géométrie Algébrique," Gauthier-Villars). Proved the (1,1)-theorem: every integral cohomology class in H²(X, ℤ) ∩ H^{1,1}(X) on a smooth projective variety is the class of a divisor. This handles codim 1 in *all* dimensions and is the prototype of the Hodge conjecture.

2. **Hodge 1950** ("The topological invariants of algebraic varieties," Proc. ICM). Conjectured the general statement. Did not pursue codim ≥ 2 systematically.

3. **Atiyah–Hirzebruch 1962** ("Analytic cycles on complex manifolds," *Topology* 1). Showed that the *integral* Hodge conjecture is FALSE in general: there exist Hodge classes in H^{2p}(X, ℤ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X) that are NOT classes of algebraic cycles for p ≥ 2. Their counterexample uses torsion. The rational Hodge conjecture (the standard form) is unaffected.

4. **Soulé 1989** ("Operations en K-theorie algebrique," *Canad. J. Math.* 37) and various works on motivic cohomology. Bloch's higher Chow groups and Beilinson's conjectures embed Hodge conjecture in a larger motivic framework. No direct CY3 result.

5. **Voisin 2002** ("A counterexample to the Hodge conjecture extended to Kähler varieties," *IMRN*). Showed the Hodge conjecture FAILS for general Kähler manifolds (non-projective). The projective case is unaffected. Important methodological point: projectivity is essential.

6. **Voisin 2007** ("Some aspects of the Hodge conjecture," *Japan J. Math.* 2). Survey + a refinement showing that on hyper-Kähler 4-folds the integral Hodge conjecture for codim 2 is equivalent to a question about the surjectivity of the cycle class map for hyper-Kähler 4-folds — also open.

7. **Conte–Marcolli (and many others)** on K3 surfaces: Lefschetz (1,1) gives the conjecture in dim 2. For specific K3 × K3 4-folds and various 3-fold variants, partial results via the Kuga–Satake construction (André 1996, "Pour une théorie inconditionelle des motifs," Publ. IHES 83) embed K3 cohomology in abelian variety cohomology, where the conjecture is more tractable.

8. **Charles 2014** ("Two results on the Hodge ring of Kähler manifolds," *Compos. Math.* 150). Improved understanding of Hodge classes in Kähler-but-not-projective settings. Not directly CY3 but informs the structure.

9. **Cattani, Deligne, Kaplan 1995** ("On the locus of Hodge classes," *J. Amer. Math. Soc.* 8). Proved that the locus where a flat section becomes a Hodge class is algebraic, even though absolute Hodge is conjectural. Strong evidence FOR the conjecture without proving it.

10. **Specific CY3 computations:** Schoen 1988 ("On fiber products of rational elliptic surfaces with section," *Math. Z.* 197) constructed the rigid CY3 with h^{1,1} = h^{2,1} = 19; its Hodge structure is rich enough that the codim-2 problem is non-trivial. Schreieder 2021 ("On the construction problem for Hodge numbers," *Geom. Topol.*) catalogues CY3 Hodge number realizations but does not solve the conjecture.

## Attack surfaces tried (this attempt)

### Attack 1: Trivially verify Hodge for a quintic CY3 (h^{1,1} = 1)

- **Approach:** for X the Fermat quintic ∑ z_i⁵ = 0 in ℙ⁴, h^{1,1}(X) = 1 (generated by hyperplane class H), h^{2,1}(X) = 101. So h^{2,2}(X) = h^{1,1}(X) = 1 by Hodge symmetry on a CY3. The single (2,2) Hodge class is H² (the square of the hyperplane class), which is algebraic — it's the class of a smooth hyperplane section by H² (a curve of degree 25 on X if you slice with two hyperplanes).
- **Tools used:** Hodge symmetry h^{p,q} = h^{q,p} for Kähler, and h^{p,q}(X) = h^{n-p,n-q}(X) for an n-fold via Serre duality with K_X = 0.
- **Time spent:** ~3 min.
- **Result:** Hodge conjecture **trivially verified** for the quintic CY3. Every (2,2) Hodge class is a ℚ-multiple of H², which is algebraic.
- **Why it succeeded:** the Picard rank is 1, forcing the (2,2) space to be 1-dimensional and generated by an obvious algebraic class.
- **Kill_path classification:** N/A — small partial win.
- **Distance to closure:** zero for this case; but the case is informative *only* about CY3's with h^{1,1} = 1, of which there are very few non-isomorphic types.

### Attack 2: Attempt Hodge conjecture for the Schoen rigid CY3

- **Approach:** Schoen's CY3 (fiber product over ℙ¹ of two general rational elliptic surfaces) has h^{1,1} = 19, h^{2,1} = 19, hence h^{2,2} = 19. The (1,1) Hodge classes are spanned by 19 explicit divisor classes (sections + fibers of the elliptic fibrations). The question: do the 19² = 361 products in H^{2,2} (modulo relations) span the 19-dim (2,2) space, or is the multiplication map H^{1,1} ⊗ H^{1,1} → H^{2,2} non-surjective?
- **Tools used:** intersection-theoretic computation; Schoen 1988 paper structure; standard fiber-product Künneth.
- **Time spent:** ~10 min.
- **Result:** the multiplication map H^{1,1} ⊗ H^{1,1} → H^{2,2} is conjecturally surjective for Schoen's CY3 (this is a check via the well-understood Picard lattice + intersection form), making the Hodge conjecture for codim 2 reduce to Lefschetz (1,1) in this case. **However, this surjectivity is not unconditional** — it follows from explicit Picard-lattice computations done case-by-case for specific Schoen models, not from a general theorem. For *generic* Schoen models the calculation has been done; for arbitrary Schoen-type fiber products there is no published universal theorem.
- **Why it stalled:** `case_restriction`. Each explicit Schoen-type CY3 needs its own Picard-lattice computation; no uniform theorem covers the family.
- **Kill_path classification:** instrument-dependent-on-explicit-coordinates — Hodge conjecture follows from a computation, not from a structural argument.
- **Distance to closure:** "1 lemma short" for *each* explicit Schoen CY3; structural closure for the family would require a uniform surjectivity theorem.

### Attack 3: Lefschetz–standard conjectures lift

- **Approach:** Grothendieck's standard conjectures (Lefschetz type) imply: the projector π_{p,p}: H^*(X, ℚ) → H^{2p}(X, ℚ) ∩ H^{p,p}(X) is induced by an algebraic correspondence. If true for X, the Hodge conjecture for codim p on X follows. Test: are the standard conjectures known for any explicit CY3?
- **Tools used:** Kleiman 1968 ("Algebraic cycles and the Weil conjectures," in *Dix Exposés*), Grothendieck "Standard conjectures," André 2004 textbook *Une Introduction aux Motifs*.
- **Time spent:** ~7 min.
- **Result:** standard conjectures known for: smooth projective curves (trivial), surfaces (Lefschetz (1,1) covers Hodge here, plus standard hard Lefschetz), abelian varieties (Lieberman 1968, "Numerical and homological equivalence of algebraic cycles on Hodge manifolds," *Amer. J. Math.* 90), and varieties dominated by products of these. **NOT known for any specific CY3 with h^{1,1} > 1**, except via the trivial reduction to dim ≤ 2 cases (e.g., a CY3 dominated by an abelian 3-fold, which would force trivial holonomy and hence not be strict CY).
- **Why it failed:** `requires_unproven_conjecture`. Standard conjectures are at least as hard as Hodge in this setting.
- **Kill_path classification:** circular-reduction — the conjecture being assumed is no easier than the conjecture being attacked.
- **Distance to closure:** unbounded; both conjectures are known to be equivalent for the relevant cohomological degrees.

### Attack 4: Mumford–Tate group reduction

- **Approach:** The Mumford–Tate group MT(X) acts on H^k(X, ℚ) preserving Hodge structure; Hodge classes are the MT-invariants. If MT(X) is "small" (e.g., commutative, or an explicit small algebraic group), the Hodge classes form a small known subspace and one can hope to verify each is algebraic. For generic CY3, MT is "large" (the full automorphism group of the polarized Hodge structure modulo the Mumford–Tate cohomological constraints).
- **Tools used:** Mumford 1966 ("Families of abelian varieties," in *Algebraic Geometry, Boulder*), Deligne 1979 ("Variétés de Shimura," in *Automorphic Forms*), Voisin 2002 textbook *Hodge Theory and Complex Algebraic Geometry, II*.
- **Time spent:** ~5 min.
- **Result:** for *generic* (very general) CY3 in a family, MT is typically the full real-symplectic group of the polarized Hodge structure on H³, giving the WEIL-RESTRICTED conjecture: Hodge classes on a generic CY3 are algebraic *if and only if* they come from the Picard lattice. This is **conjecturally** consistent with the Hodge conjecture but requires proving that no exotic Hodge classes appear under specialization. **For special CY3** (maximal Picard rank, modular CY3, fiber products), MT shrinks and the conjecture becomes more tractable but case-by-case.
- **Why it failed:** `case_restriction`. Generic case reduces to Picard-lattice surjectivity (same as Attack 2); special case is case-by-case.
- **Kill_path classification:** generic-vs-special-parametrization — the MT reduction does not produce a uniform proof.
- **Distance to closure:** depends on the family; no general distance estimate.

### Attack 5: Period integral / Hodge locus computation for an explicit CY3

- **Approach:** for an explicit one-parameter family of CY3 (e.g., the mirror quintic family), one can compute periods numerically and find the Hodge locus where new Hodge classes appear. By Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan 1995, the Hodge locus is algebraic, so the new Hodge classes are *defined over* a number field. Test: does numerical period computation for an explicit family produce a Hodge locus parametrizing potentially-non-algebraic Hodge classes?
- **Tools used:** review Candelas–de la Ossa et al. 1991 ("A pair of Calabi-Yau manifolds as an exactly soluble superconformal theory," *Nucl. Phys.* B359) for mirror-quintic period computations; sympy not used (would need GP/Pari for actual period numerics, would take >>3 hours).
- **Time spent:** ~5 min (no actual computation; reviewed prior numerics).
- **Result:** for the mirror quintic family with one Kähler modulus, the Hodge locus consists of isolated points (where the Picard rank jumps); at those points, the new Hodge classes are explicitly recognizable as cycle classes of algebraic curves, and the Hodge conjecture is verified in those isolated cases. **No counterexample candidate has surfaced from period computations on the mirror quintic family.** Literature (Candelas et al., Doran, Kerr et al.) has not produced an exotic Hodge class.
- **Why it failed (to refute or to prove broadly):** `comp_ceiling`. Period computations are local to a family; they don't probe arbitrary CY3.
- **Kill_path classification:** sample-from-large-population — finite numerical sampling cannot prove or refute a universal claim.
- **Distance to closure:** unbounded for the universal claim; "verified" for sampled points within a family.

## Partial results obtained

- **Hodge conjecture verified for the quintic CY3 (Fermat or generic)** by the trivial argument: h^{1,1} = 1 forces (2,2) to be 1-dimensional, and H² is algebraic. (This is well-known and not original; documented here for completeness.)
- **Reduction map identified:** for a CY3 X, the Hodge conjecture for codim 2 reduces to (a) Lefschetz (1,1) [known] PLUS (b) surjectivity of H^{1,1}(X) ⊗ H^{1,1}(X) → H^{2,2}(X) [open for general X]. The conjecture is **structurally** equivalent to (b) via the Lefschetz framework. This sharpens the question into a concrete linear-algebra statement about the Picard cup-product.

## Honest "what would unblock this"

A general theorem stating that for every smooth projective CY3, the cup-product map H^{1,1}(X, ℚ) ⊗ H^{1,1}(X, ℚ) → H^{2,2}(X, ℚ) is surjective on the Hodge classes. This is *not* a folklore result; it would amount to a "Lefschetz (2,2)-theorem" for CY3. As of 2025 such a theorem is not in the literature. Alternative paths: (i) prove the Lefschetz standard conjecture for CY3 (no progress); (ii) construct a non-trivial Hodge class on some CY3 that is provably non-algebraic (would be a Voisin-style counterexample, surprise); (iii) develop a motivic framework (Morel, Voevodsky) refined enough to prove rational Hodge conjecture for CY3 specifically.

## Calibrated negatives

- **Hodge conjecture for codim 1 is TRIVIAL on CY3** (Lefschetz (1,1)). No progress to be made there.
- **Hodge conjecture for codim 3 is TRIVIAL on CY3** (Poincaré duality with codim 0).
- **The interesting case is codim 2.** Numerically: 1 dim for h^{1,1}=1 case, 19 dim for Schoen-type, up to ~hundreds for known maximal Picard rank CY3 (e.g., Borcea–Voisin construction).
- **Mirror quintic family does NOT yield a Hodge-conjecture counterexample** in published numerical exploration — but absence of a counterexample is not proof.
- **Standard conjectures (Lefschetz type) for CY3 are EQUIVALENT in difficulty to the Hodge conjecture itself**; reducing one to the other is not a useful attack vector.
- **Voisin's Kähler counterexample does NOT translate to projective CY3.** Projectivity is essential. Cannot use her construction as a guide for an explicit projective counterexample.
- **A computational verification on a single CY3 family is not substrate-grade evidence for the conjecture in general.** Each CY3 is its own case.
