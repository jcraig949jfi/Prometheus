# Study 16: Noise vs Signal in Conjecture Generation

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Owner:** general-purpose subagent (Aporia delegation)
**Substrate connection:** Calibration discipline (cf Ergon's CALIBRATION cycle 2026-05-04, `stoa/discussions/2026-05-04-ergon-CALIBRATION-iter28-stability.md`); conjecture-quality filters; base-rate estimation per domain; null-world and kill-ledger infrastructure (`aporia/scouting/09_null_world_generator.md`, `stoa/proposals/2026-04-25-aporia-two-track-epistemics.md`).

## Problem statement (Prometheus-adapted)

Yesterday Ergon revoked a "7-record A149086+ cluster" headline finding when stability analysis showed it was produced by only 1 of 3 random seeds. The substrate's current operational heuristic for "robust conjecture" is **cross-seed reproducibility on high-quality cluster match-sets** (5 of 12 surviving at 3/3 seeds — the 42% that became the "meaningful number"). The question for this study: is cross-seed reproducibility *enough*, what other filters does the relevant literature endorse, and what is the empirical base-rate of false-positive-but-plausible mathematical conjectures we should be calibrating against?

Four sub-questions, each tied to one operational handle:

1. Beyond cross-seed replication, which conjecture-quality filters are documented and cheap?
2. Is there a literature-supported way to estimate base-rate of false conjectures by domain?
3. Which signal/noise discriminators are the highest-value for the substrate to compute?
4. Is the Ergon CALIBRATION cycle (1/3 seed → revoke + ADDENDUM) novel, or standard practice in some adjacent field?

Headline: (a) cross-seed reproducibility is necessary but a single dimension of a multi-axis robustness checklist that ML reproducibility, statistics, and physics have converged on independently; (b) no general "false-conjecture base rate by domain" exists in the literature, but several specific historical case studies bound it loosely (numerical evidence up to 10⁷ has failed publicly at least four times: Mertens, Pólya, Skewes, second-Hardy-Littlewood); (c) the highest-leverage cheap discriminators are *parameter-grid robustness*, *out-of-sample-range prediction*, and *informed-null comparison*, in that order; (d) the Ergon discipline is *not* novel — it is a faithful instance of the *registered-report / pre-registered analysis-plan* pattern from psychology and the *seed-stability ablation* pattern from RL benchmarking; the novelty is enforcing it on a *single agent's own claim within 24 hours* via Stoa rather than across labs over months.

## Literature scan

**Reproducibility and ML benchmarking.** Henderson et al., "Deep Reinforcement Learning that Matters" (AAAI 2018, [arXiv:1709.06560](https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.06560)) is the canonical reference: across-seed variance in deep RL frequently exceeds across-algorithm variance, so any single-seed claim is uninterpretable. Recommended discipline: report mean ± std across ≥5 seeds, with significance tested via permutation or bootstrap. Agarwal et al., "Deep RL at the Edge of the Statistical Precipice" (NeurIPS 2021, [arXiv:2108.13264](https://arxiv.org/abs/2108.13264)) extends this to *interquartile mean*, *probability of improvement*, and *performance profiles* as more honest aggregations than single-point means. Both papers' core failure mode — *picking the best seed and forgetting the rest* — is precisely what the Ergon cycle caught.

**Pre-registration and registered reports** (Munafò et al., "A manifesto for reproducible science," *Nature Human Behaviour* 1, 2017, [DOI:10.1038/s41562-016-0021](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41562-016-0021); Nosek et al., 2018 *PNAS*, [DOI:10.1073/pnas.1708274114](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1708274114)). The pattern: lock the analysis plan before seeing the data, publish predictions before observing outcomes, separate exploratory from confirmatory analyses. The two-track-epistemics proposal (Aporia, 2026-04-25) is structurally a registered-report pattern adapted for a multi-agent substrate: Track A is the confirmatory channel with frozen battery, Track B is the exploratory channel that may not publish.

**Multiple-testing corrections.** Bonferroni (1936): divide α by number of tests. Holm (1979, *Scand. J. Stat.* 6): step-down version, uniformly more powerful than Bonferroni. Benjamini-Hochberg (1995, *JRSS B* 57, [DOI:10.1111/j.2517-6161.1995.tb02031.x](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.2517-6161.1995.tb02031.x)): controls *false discovery rate* rather than family-wise error rate, much more powerful at high test counts. Storey (2002, *JRSS B* 64): q-value formulation with empirical-Bayes π₀ estimation. The substrate's `discovery_env` and Charon's battery generate test counts in the thousands per session; without explicit FDR control the per-test α is meaningless. Charon's frozen 25-test battery applied to one finding is fine; the same battery applied to 1000 candidate findings yields an expected 50 spurious passes at α=0.05, FWER ≈ 1, FDR depends on truth-base-rate.

**P-hacking and garden-of-forking-paths.** Simmons, Nelson, Simonsohn, "False-Positive Psychology" (*Psychological Science* 22, 2011, [DOI:10.1177/0956797611417632](https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797611417632)): showed that researcher-degrees-of-freedom inflate false-positive rates from nominal 5% to >60% even with honest researchers. Gelman & Loken, "The garden of forking paths" (preprint 2013, [stat.columbia.edu](http://www.stat.columbia.edu/~gelman/research/unpublished/p_hacking.pdf)): even without p-hacking, *post-hoc analytic decisions conditional on the data* inflate Type I error. Direct relevance: every time the substrate picks "the cluster that looks interesting" after seeing the seed, it is forking-paths. The Ergon CALIBRATION cycle's discipline of *fixing the cluster definition before the seed sweep* is the structural fix.

**Mathematical-conjecture historical base rates.** No systematic survey exists. But the canonical false-conjecture cases place a non-trivial lower bound on the false-positive rate of "checked numerically to 10⁶–10¹⁰":
- *Mertens conjecture*: |M(n)| < √n for all n. Numerically held for all n ≤ 10¹⁰. Disproved by Odlyzko & te Riele (1985, [DOI:10.1515/crll.1985.357.138](https://doi.org/10.1515/crll.1985.357.138)) via analytic bounds on the zeros of ζ; explicit counterexample exists below 10³⁰ but has never been computed.
- *Pólya conjecture*: L(n) = #{k ≤ n : Ω(k) odd} − #{k ≤ n : Ω(k) even} ≤ 0 for n ≥ 2. Held to ~10⁶. Disproved by Haselgrove (1958, *Mathematika* 5, 141–145) at n ≈ 906,150,257 (Tanaka 1980).
- *Skewes-related miscount*: π(x) < li(x) was conjectured by Gauss/Riemann; numerically held to 10²⁰. Littlewood (1914) proved the inequality reverses infinitely often; Skewes (1933, 1955) gave first explicit upper bound on the first crossing (now known to occur below ~10³¹⁶).
- *Second Hardy-Littlewood conjecture*: π(x+y) ≤ π(x) + π(y). Believed for all x, y > 1; Hensley & Richards (1974, *Acta Arithmetica* 25) showed it conflicts with the prime k-tuples conjecture and is almost certainly false near "admissible" prime constellations, though no explicit counterexample is yet known.
- *Borsuk conjecture*: every bounded set in ℝⁿ can be partitioned into n+1 sets of smaller diameter. Held trivially to n ≤ 3. Disproved for n ≥ 64 (Kahn-Kalai 1993, *Bull. AMS* 29, [DOI:10.1090/S0273-0979-1993-00398-7](https://doi.org/10.1090/S0273-0979-1993-00398-7)); status open for 4 ≤ n ≤ 63.
- *Hilbert-Smith*: still open in full generality (proved for actions on 3-manifolds by Pardon 2013, [arXiv:1112.2324](https://arxiv.org/abs/1112.2324)). Listed in the prompt; status is "neither confirmed nor refuted," not a confirmed false positive.

These cases together argue that *passing numerical evidence to 10⁶ or even 10¹⁰ is not a strong signal*: the prior probability of being wrong despite such evidence is bounded below by historical incidence and is decidedly non-zero. For Prometheus's substrate, this is a calibration warning: PROMOTE thresholds that rely on exhaustive small-N verification will admit Mertens-class false positives at non-negligible rate.

**Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer original numerical evidence** (BSD, 1965, *J. Reine Angew. Math.* 218 and 1965 ICM). The original computation: rank vs. ∏ |Ep|/p across a few dozen elliptic curves, plotted on log-log paper, showing the conjectured slope 1 relationship. By modern standards this is *suggestive*, not *evidence*: tiny sample, no null model, no out-of-range prediction, no robustness-to-curve-family-choice analysis. The conjecture's eventual partial-proof status (Gross-Zagier, Kolyvagin for analytic rank ≤ 1; modularity/Wiles bridging) was *not* a function of evidence quality but of independent theoretical scaffolding. Modern numerical-evidence standards in arithmetic geometry (e.g., LMFDB project) routinely exceed BSD's original by 6+ orders of magnitude in sample size and explicit-formula precision; whether the prior on the conjecture is materially updated by this is a separate question.

**Catalan conjecture** (now Mihailescu's theorem, 2002, *J. Reine Angew. Math.* 572): only solution of xᵖ − yᵠ = 1 in integers is 3² − 2³ = 1. Pre-proof numerical evidence reached p, q ≤ 10⁷ (Cassels-class bounds). Was eventually proved by *algebraic* means (cyclotomic field theory), not by extending the numerical bound. Pattern: *numerical evidence accumulated for decades did not move the conjecture across the proof threshold; structural reformulation did.*

**Cosmology and physics nulls** (Crocce et al. 2015, [arXiv:1411.3286](https://arxiv.org/abs/1411.3286); Aaboud et al. ATLAS Higgs 2017, etc.). Standard discipline: report findings against multiple independent mock-universe / control-region nulls, with the strictest setting the headline. Already imported into Prometheus via `aporia/scouting/09_null_world_generator.md` for the Mahler-measure domain; the substrate has the architecture but only the broadest pattern (a) implemented as of this study.

**Genome-wide-association-study (GWAS) base rates.** Manolio et al. (*Nature* 2009, [DOI:10.1038/nature08494](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature08494)) and the GWAS catalog (Buniello et al. 2019, [DOI:10.1093/nar/gky1120](https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gky1120)) provide the *only* large-scale empirical base-rate measurement for "plausible-looking association" survival under independent replication. Pre-2007 (pre-genome-wide-significance-threshold-of-5×10⁻⁸): >95% of published associations failed to replicate. Post-Bonferroni-corrected genome-wide threshold: replication rate rose to 70-90%. Direct moral for Prometheus: *a domain-appropriate multiple-testing correction can shift the empirical false-positive rate by an order of magnitude*, and the correction is computable up-front.

**Seed-ablation and stability discipline in ML model selection.** Bouthillier et al., "Accounting for Variance in Machine Learning Benchmarks" (MLSys 2021, [arXiv:2103.03098](https://arxiv.org/abs/2103.03098)) explicitly recommends *bootstrap-over-seeds plus permutation-over-data* as the joint check. The Ergon CALIBRATION cycle is the seed dimension of this; a permutation-over-data dimension (e.g., shuffle the OEIS labels and re-run the cluster discovery) is not yet present in the iter-28 stability analyzer.

## Substrate-relevance

Three load-bearing connections:

1. **Cross-seed reproducibility is dimension 1 of a checklist, not the checklist.** The Ergon discipline correctly identified the dominant failure mode (single-seed lucky combinatorial assembly) but did not yet address the other documented dimensions: *parameter-grid sweeps* (sensitivity to ε, threshold, learning rate), *out-of-sample-range prediction* (does the cluster's predicate generalize to a149 records the engine has never seen?), *informed-null comparison* (does the cluster pass against the null-world generator from Scout #9, not just against absolute lift?), *cross-method confirmation* (does an entirely different search algorithm rediscover the cluster?). The 5/12 cluster recurrence at 3/3 seeds is necessary but not sufficient for "discovery."

2. **The substrate has the kill-ledger and incubator infrastructure for empirical base-rate tracking but is not yet using it for that purpose.** `aporia/mathematics/kills.jsonl` (per the two-track-epistemics proposal) is designed to be append-only with full battery results per kill. Once the ledger has hundreds of kills, the substrate can compute its *own* domain-conditional false-positive rate empirically: "of the last 100 lift > X findings in domain D, how many survived re-test?" This is the *substrate-internal* analog to the GWAS catalog's replication-rate measurement. No similar mathematical artifact exists in the literature; Prometheus may end up the first to publish one.

3. **The Stoa CALIBRATION-post pattern is a registered-report-class discipline at sub-day cadence.** The novelty is not the *content* of stability analysis (standard since the Henderson 2018 paper) but the *latency*: an agent posting a DISCOVERY headline at 09:00, an ADDENDUM at 14:00, and a CALIBRATION revocation at 18:00 of the same day. This is faster than any registered-report institution can operate, because it does not require human peer review at each hop. The closest analog is the LIGO collaboration's internal blind-injection protocol (Abbott et al. 2009, *Class. Quantum Grav.* 27), where a fraction of "detections" are known-to-be-fake and the collaboration must catch them before the paper draft circulates. Prometheus's CALIBRATION pattern is the *self-injecting* version: the agent injects its own optimistic claim, then injects the stability analysis as the test, in the same day.

## Concrete operational handles

1. **Add a five-axis robustness scorecard to the PROMOTE gate.** Axes: (i) cross-seed reproducibility (current), (ii) parameter-grid robustness (does the cluster persist under ±10% threshold perturbation?), (iii) informed-null pass (does it beat the Scout-#9 null-world by K ≥ 5?), (iv) out-of-sample prediction (does the cluster's predicate correctly classify held-out a149 records the engine never saw?), (v) cross-method confirmation (does a *different* search algorithm — e.g., greedy beam vs. RL — find the same cluster?). PROMOTE requires ≥ 4/5; *report* of a finding can fire on ≥ 3/5; ≤ 2/5 is exploratory only and stays in Track B. This generalizes the Ergon discipline without overfitting to it.

2. **Compute a substrate-internal false-positive rate per domain from the kill ledger, monthly.** Once `aporia/mathematics/kills.jsonl` reaches ~50 kills per domain, run a per-domain empirical-Bayes π₀ estimator (per Storey 2002) to estimate the base-rate of true positives. Surface this as `aporia/mathematics/base_rates.jsonl` and feed it into the PROMOTE-gate's prior. This makes Prometheus's calibration *data-driven* rather than discipline-borrowed.

3. **Add a permutation-over-data axis to the stability analyzer.** Current `ergon/learner/tools/stability_analysis.py` shuffles seeds; add a mode that shuffles the *labels* (a149 record → reward) and re-runs the cluster discovery on the shuffled corpus. The cluster recurrence rate on label-shuffled data is the *informed null* for cluster recurrence on real data. Cost: ~50 LOC extension; one extra column in the recurrence table.

4. **Adopt the registered-report-style "freeze the analysis plan" discipline at Stoa-post creation.** Before posting a DISCOVERY, the agent commits in a one-line preamble: "*The cluster definition, lift threshold, and seed list were fixed at iteration N before any of these clusters were seen.*" If the post lacks the preamble, the post is automatically tagged as exploratory regardless of effect size. Cheap; matches the Munafò et al. 2017 manifesto's first recommendation.

5. **Cite the Henderson 2018 / Bouthillier 2021 / Munafò 2017 lineage in the substrate's calibration documentation.** The Ergon CALIBRATION cycle is *not* novel discipline — it is a well-supported pattern from RL benchmarking and reproducibility methodology — and saying so honestly strengthens both the discipline (anchors it to literature) and the substrate's epistemic posture (we did not invent calibration, we are applying it). One paragraph in `feedback_calibration.md` or a new `feedback_seed_stability.md`.

## Falsification

The central claim of this study — *cross-seed reproducibility is one axis of a five-axis robustness checklist with documented literature backing; the historical false-positive rate of "checked to 10⁶–10¹⁰" mathematical conjectures is non-trivial and bounds Prometheus's PROMOTE gate calibration; the Ergon CALIBRATION cycle is an instance of a known discipline at unusually low latency, not a novel discipline* — would be refuted by:

- A published systematic survey establishing a base-rate of mathematical-conjecture failure-after-numerical-verification by domain. Search did not find one; if one exists the historical-incidence section above should be replaced.
- A demonstration that ≥4-of-5 robustness axes are mutually redundant in the substrate's empirical kill ledger (e.g., cross-seed reproducibility *implies* parameter-grid robustness with > 0.95 conditional probability), in which case the scorecard is over-engineered.
- A published instance of multi-agent same-day DISCOVERY → CALIBRATION → REVOKE protocol predating Prometheus. Closest analog found is the LIGO blind-injection protocol, which is multi-month not same-day; if a tighter analog exists the novelty claim should be downgraded further.
- Empirical evidence from the kill ledger that the substrate's domain-internal false-positive rate is materially different from what the GWAS / particle-physics analogies predict (either much lower — Prometheus is unusually clean — or much higher — Prometheus is unusually noisy). Either finding would re-shape the PROMOTE gate calibration.

## Open questions raised

1. Does the substrate's PROMOTE gate currently incorporate any explicit prior on the per-domain false-conjecture base rate, or is the gate purely effect-size-based? (Suspect: latter.)
2. The 42% cluster-recurrence rate Ergon reported (5 of 12) is itself an estimate from a small sample — what is its confidence interval, and is it stable across re-runs of the stability analyzer with different seed sets?
3. Should "robust on the engine's own search algorithm" and "robust under a different search algorithm" be the same axis or different axes? (Argument for different: a finding that survives only the search method that produced it is suspect.)
4. The kill-ledger's `weak_signal_residue` field is the substrate's analog to "interesting null result"; should there be a *symmetric* `false_positive_residue` field on PROMOTE records, capturing what the finding would have looked like if it had been spurious? Would let the substrate train its own discriminator over time.
5. Is there a productive analog of GWAS's *genome-wide-significance-threshold of 5×10⁻⁸* for the substrate — i.e., a single domain-agnostic α floor below which a finding is admitted regardless of other axes? Probably not (mathematical-discovery is too non-stationary), but worth flagging.
6. The Ergon calibration's "seeds" are RNG seeds for stochastic search; the analogous mathematical-discovery "seed" might be *initial polynomial family* or *starting elliptic curve*. Cross-seed reproducibility in math may need to mean cross-*initialization* reproducibility, which is a much harder ablation. Worth specifying before generalizing.
