# Study 01: Minimal Generative Bases Across Mathematical Domains

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Owner:** general-purpose subagent (Aporia delegation)
**Substrate connection:** Whether Prometheus's 85-op `arsenal_meta` primitive set is the right size and shape for cross-domain mathematical coverage.

## Problem statement (Prometheus-adapted)

Prometheus exposes ~85 typed mathematical operations through `prometheus_math.arsenal_meta.ARSENAL_REGISTRY` (confirmed in `ergon/learner/genome.py:11` and `prometheus_math/ARSENAL.md`), plus 7 substrate opcodes in the Sigma kernel (RESOLVE, CLAIM, FALSIFY, GATE, PROMOTE, ERRATA, TRACE) and BIND/EVAL for callable binding. The substrate-design question: is 85 too many, too few, or right? Is there a literature-backed "universal minimal basis" that would either compress this set or flag missing load-bearing primitives?

The relevant prior work splits into three traditions: (a) **logical minimal bases** (axiom systems: ZFC, ETCS, HoTT, Tarski-Grothendieck, CIC), (b) **structural minimal bases** (Bourbaki mother structures, Lawvere theories, universal-algebra clones), and (c) **empirical minimal bases** (mathlib tactic frequencies, Wiedijk's "100 theorems" cross-prover comparison, ProofGraph dependency-graph analysis). These three traditions answer different questions and the operational gap between (a) and (c) is the most important finding for Prometheus.

## Literature scan

**Logical minimal bases.** ZFC fits on a card (~9 schemes); ETCS (Lawvere 1964) replaces it with axioms about the category of sets — undefined terms reduce to mappings, domain, codomain, composition. ETCS is widely accepted as foundationally adequate for "much of number theory, elementary analysis, and algebra." HoTT/UF (Univalent Foundations Program 2013, arXiv:1308.0729) reduces primitives further to dependent type theory plus the univalence axiom and higher inductive types. Lawvere theories generalize this: every algebraic structure is a finite-product-preserving functor from some Lawvere theory T to Set. None of these systems claims that their *axiomatic* minimality translates to a *generative* minimality — they tell you what the floor is, not what you actually need to do mathematics.

**Structural minimal bases.** Bourbaki (1939+) identified three "mother structures" — algebraic, order, topological — sometimes glossed as Combination, Comparison, Continuity (Corry, *Modern Algebra and the Rise of Mathematical Structures*, 2nd ed. 2004, ch. 7; Bell, "Reflections on Bourbaki's Notion of Structure"). All "multiple structures" (topological algebra, ordered fields, etc.) are layered on top. This is the canonical historical answer to the cross-field-basis question and it is *small* (3, not 85). However, Bourbaki's structures are types, not operations — they are closer to sorts in a typed language than to executable primitives.

**Universal-algebra clones.** Rosenberg's classification of maximal/minimal clones on finite sets (Rosenberg 1986, recently reviewed arXiv:2406.15184) gives a precise theory of "minimal generating sets of operations" for a fixed carrier. There are finitely many minimal clones on any finite set; on a 2-element set, all Boolean operations are generated by NAND alone (a single ternary primitive in the relational reduct case). This is the cleanest mathematical formalization of "minimal generative basis," but it generalizes poorly to mathematics-as-a-whole because the underlying carrier changes (integers, manifolds, knots, schemes), and clone theory does not address how to *cross* carriers.

**Empirical bases (Lean mathlib, Coq, Mizar).** Wiedijk's "100 theorems" survey (Notices AMS 2008) compares formalizations across HOL Light, Isabelle, Coq/Rocq, Mizar, Lean, Metamath. Six systems now exceed 70% coverage. ProofGraph (proofgraph.org) has extracted full mathlib4: **323,489 declarations and 7,516,850 dependency edges**, with planned centrality / community-detection analysis — but published centrality rankings of definitions are **not yet available** (no canonical source identified for a definitive top-N most-cited mathlib defs). For tactic frequency: ML4PG (Komendantskaya et al., arXiv:1212.3618) reports that of the ~100 Coq tactics, only ~10 are common; SSReflect uses fewer than 10. Mathlib4 has hundreds of tactics catalogued (haruhisa-enomoto/mathlib4-all-tactics), but no peer-reviewed empirical Pareto-distribution paper specific to Lean 4 mathlib was identified in this scan. The qualitative consensus across proof-assistant communities is that **a small kernel of tactics (rewrite/simp, intro, apply, exact, induction, cases, ring/field_simp, linarith/polyrith, decide, ext) accounts for the vast majority of proof steps**, with a long tail of specialized tactics for specific subdomains.

**Cross-system comparisons.** Wiedijk's "Statistics on Digital Libraries of Mathematics" (2007) and follow-ups across HOL Light / Isabelle/HOL / Coq / Mizar found rough convergence on what gets formalized first (real analysis, basic algebra, set theory) but **divergence on which primitives are taken as foundational** — Mizar uses Tarski-Grothendieck, HOL Light uses higher-order logic with choice, Coq/Rocq uses CIC. This is direct evidence that the *logical* basis is not unique even when the *theorems proved* converge.

## Substrate-relevance

Three load-bearing connections to Prometheus:

1. **The 85-op count sits in a defensible band.** ML4PG-style empirical evidence suggests ~10 dominant operations per proof-assistant tactic vocabulary, with a long tail; Prometheus's 85 is consistent with this if you assume Prometheus needs to span ~8 categories (AI, CAS, COMB, DB, NT, NUM, OPT, SAT, TOP per `ARSENAL.md`) at roughly 10 ops each. So **85 is not obviously inflated**; it is on the order of "10 dominant ops × number-of-domains."

2. **Bourbaki's mother structures are sorts, not ops, and Prometheus is missing this layer.** The arsenal exposes operations grouped by *backend category* (NT, TOP, COMB), which is a tooling axis, not a mathematical-structure axis. Bourbaki suggests there is a more invariant axis (algebraic / order / topological) that cuts across backends. A `class_number` (NT) and `hyperbolic_volume` (TOP) might both be "algebraic invariants" by Bourbaki's lens, but Prometheus has no operator that knows this. The Sigma kernel's 7 opcodes are control-flow, not structural — they don't fill this gap.

3. **The logical/generative distinction is real and Prometheus is on the generative side.** ETCS/HoTT minimize axioms (logical minimal basis: ~5–10 axioms). Prometheus's 85 ops are a generative basis (ops that build interesting examples). These are categorically different and the literature does not give one universal number bridging them. The relevant Prometheus question is therefore *not* "can we get below 85?" but "do our 85 cover the operations the substrate's actual workflows need, and are their compositions closed under the Sigma kernel?"

## Concrete operational handles

1. **Empirical self-audit, not a-priori reduction.** Run a centrality analysis on Prometheus's own usage logs (Ergon trial ledgers, `arsenal_meta` call counts in `ergon/learner/trials/`, residual classification in `sigma_kernel/residuals.py`). The literature predicts a Pareto distribution: ~10 ops will dominate, ~30 will be common, ~45 will be vestigial-or-specialized. If your distribution looks flat, the arsenal is under-typed (everything looks equally useful because nothing is being preferred). If a long tail of zero-call ops exists, those are candidates for deprecation or for "missing problem types that would exercise them."

2. **Add a Bourbaki-axis tag to each `arsenal_meta` op.** Three values: `algebraic`, `order`, `topological`, plus `mixed`. This is metadata, ~30 minutes of human work, costs nothing at runtime. Once tagged, you can ask: *do bridges discovered by Charon / Agora cross Bourbaki axes more than they cross backend categories?* If yes, the Bourbaki axis is a better routing prior than the current backend grouping. This is a concrete, falsifiable substrate experiment.

3. **Identify likely-missing load-bearing primitives by cross-referencing mathlib top-N tactics with `arsenal_meta`.** Plausible gaps Prometheus may underweight (based on tactic-vocabulary commonality across proof assistants — these are *hypotheses for audit*, not confirmed gaps): explicit `rewrite_under_equation` / term-rewriting, `induct_on` (well-founded induction as an operator, not just a Python loop), `case_split` / `decide` (case enumeration with closure), `ext` (extensionality reduction), `coerce` (typed coercion between equivalent representations). Several may already exist under different names; an arsenal-vs-mathlib-tactic cross-walk would clarify.

4. **Distinguish "minimal" from "merely sufficient" by ablation.** The clean test: remove op X from `arsenal_meta`, re-run a fixed benchmark of substrate workflows (e.g., 50 Ergon trials on a frozen task set), measure performance delta. If delta ≈ 0, X is not minimal-load-bearing. This is the same logic as the kill_vector primitive (`prometheus_math/KILL_VECTOR_SPEC.md`) but applied to the arsenal itself. Without this experiment, "minimal" is a label, not a claim.

5. **Resist the urge to compress to <10 primitives.** Logical minimal bases (ETCS, HoTT) achieve ~5–10 axioms but only by pushing all complexity into derived definitions. The cost is *enormous proof-step inflation* — Metamath's 100 theorems require ~3014 intermediate theorems just to construct the complex numbers from ZFC. A substrate that compressed `arsenal_meta` to 10 primitives would replicate this inflation in its action space and likely make Ergon's RL search intractable. The current ~85-op band is probably near a sweet spot for *generative* (not logical) minimality.

## Falsification

The central operational claim of this study — *85 ops is in a defensible band, and "minimal generative basis" is a different question from "minimal logical basis"* — would be refuted by any of the following:

- A Prometheus usage audit shows a flat distribution with no Pareto tail (would suggest the arsenal is doing nothing distinctive — operations are not being preferred over one another).
- A documented research project (which this scan did not find) demonstrates an empirical universal minimal basis of <20 ops that regenerates equivalent breadth of math experimentally, not just axiomatically.
- An ablation experiment shows that >50% of `arsenal_meta` ops can be removed with no performance loss on a representative substrate task set — this would mean 85 is bloated and the real working basis is much smaller.

## Open questions raised

1. Has anyone published the actual top-N most-cited definitions / most-used tactics for mathlib4? ProofGraph has the data extracted but the centrality analysis appears unpublished as of this scan.
2. Is there a concrete mapping from Bourbaki's three mother structures to the 85 `arsenal_meta` ops, and would such a tagging change Charon's bridge-discovery success rate?
3. What is the operational analogue of "axiom of choice" in `arsenal_meta` — is there a hidden non-constructive primitive (e.g., `solve_mip` calling SCIP) that the substrate treats as constructive but isn't? This matters for `FALSIFY` semantics.
4. Does the Sigma kernel's 7-opcode set correspond to a known minimal control algebra (e.g., the categorical-logic notion of a "doctrine"), or is it an ad hoc engineering choice that happens to be small?
5. Are knot-theory operations (`hyperbolic_volume`, `knot_floer_homology`) genuinely orthogonal primitives, or are they specializations of a more abstract "compute invariant" op that Prometheus could expose generically and dispatch by type?
