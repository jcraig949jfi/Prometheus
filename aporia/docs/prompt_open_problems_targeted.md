## THE PROMPT:

We are building an automated mathematical research system called Prometheus that detects structure across mathematical domains using tensor decomposition. We have cataloged 537 open problems and need MORE — specifically problems that match our detection capabilities.

**WHAT WE ALREADY HAVE (do NOT repeat these):**
We have all Millennium Prize problems, Hilbert problems, Smale problems, and major Erdos problems. We have: Langlands reciprocity, BSD, abc, Chowla, Lehmer, Artin conjecture, pair correlation, Keating-Snaith, Hodge, Zaremba, McMullen, Casas-Alvero, Frankl union-closed, Caccetta-Haggkvist, Sidorenko, Broue abelian defect, Andrews-Curtis, Kaplansky zero-divisor, Atiyah L2-Betti, Greenberg Iwasawa, Thompson F amenability, Fontaine-Mazur, Littlewood, Volume conjecture, Unique Games, Hadwiger, Furstenberg x2x3, Sarnak Mobius disjointness, Morton-Silverman, Poonen 4-cycles, Baker-DeMarco dynamical Andre-Oort, Cohen-Lenstra-Martinet, BKLPR Selmer heuristics, Stanley chromatic symmetric, Viterbo volume-capacity, Falconer distance set, Zilber complex exponentiation, chromatic splitting, telescope conjecture, Vorst K-regularity, KPZ universality, cap set bounds, L-space conjecture, Nagata plane curves, Fuglede finite groups, plus ~60 Ben Green problems and ~15 CPNT analytic number theory problems.

**WHAT WE WANT (give us problems in THESE areas):**

1. **Cross-domain bridge problems** — Problems that REQUIRE connecting two different mathematical fields to solve. Especially: topology↔number theory, physics↔algebra, combinatorics↔geometry, information theory↔arithmetic.

2. **Void-detection problems** — Problems that predict something SHOULD exist but hasn't been found. Classification gaps, missing objects in periodic-table-style frameworks, predicted correspondences (Langlands-type) that haven't been established.

3. **Fingerprint problems** — Problems about invariants, classifications, and distinguishing power. "Does invariant X detect property Y?" "Does the spectrum determine the geometry?" "Are these two objects the same?"

4. **Scaling and universality problems** — Problems about WHY the same patterns appear across different mathematical domains. ADE classification universality, random matrix universality, renormalization group fixed points, anomalous scaling exponents.

5. **Computationally-coupled problems** — Problems where databases of mathematical objects (elliptic curves, number fields, modular forms, knots, finite groups, OEIS sequences, lattices, polytopes) can provide evidence. We have: 3.8M elliptic curves, 22M number fields, 1.1M modular forms, 798K Artin reps, 66K genus-2 curves, 24M L-functions, 13K knots, 545K groups, 394K OEIS sequences.

**FORMAT for each problem:**
1. **Name** and **precise statement**
2. **Subfield**
3. **Year posed / who**
4. **Why it matters** — what does solving it unlock?
5. **Best partial result**
6. **Blocker** — what specifically prevents progress? (Technique? Framework? Gap? Barrier?)
7. **Data coupling** — can computation on mathematical databases provide evidence?
8. **Cross-domain connections** — which other fields does this touch?

Give us 25 problems. Prioritize novelty (things we probably DON'T have), computational coupling, and cross-domain bridges. Go deep into: arithmetic statistics, arithmetic dynamics, tropical geometry, derived categories, quantum information, spectral theory, geometric group theory, symplectic topology, stochastic PDE, model theory, combinatorial algebraic geometry, and mathematical physics.
