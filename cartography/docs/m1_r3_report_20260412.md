## 5. Key Findings

### 5.1 Knot polynomial identities dominate the knot batch

Four of the nine knot tests (G.R1.3, G.R4.6, G.R4.5, C84) reduce to the identity det = |Alexander(-1)| and related polynomial evaluation identities. These have eta^2 > 0.74 because they are mathematical tautologies, not discoveries. The "high z-scores" from prior genocide rounds were measuring known identities.

### 5.2 C67 (Alexander recurrence) is the standout new result

The original "0% killed" was a data threshold error, not a real negative. With the corrected threshold (>= 8 coefficients), **54.6% of Alexander polynomials satisfy an order-3 linear recurrence**. This is:
- Not a known tautology (F27 = NOT_TAUTOLOGY)
- Strongly predicted by determinant (eta^2 = 0.286)
- Increasing with crossing number (31% at cn=10, 56% at cn=12)
- The only result in this batch that is both structurally meaningful and not already known

This warrants deeper investigation: what is the algebraic mechanism behind the order-3 recurrence? Is it a consequence of the skein relation structure?

### 5.3 Data quality determines verdict quality

Three results changed classification after data fixes:
- **G.R1.8** went from z=3.5 SURVIVES to p=0.51 KILLED when crossing numbers went from 249 to 12,965. Small samples produce false positives.
- **G.alex** dropped from eta^2=0.084 to 0.039 with full data. Still real but weaker.
- **C67** went from "killed" to the most interesting result -- entirely due to threshold correction.

Lesson: **always verify that "no data" means "the hypothesis is wrong" rather than "the data pipeline has a gap."**

### 5.4 Cross-domain signals are negligible or mediated

The three cross-domain membership tests (G.R5.3, G.R5.nf, G.R5.sg) all survive permutation testing but have eta^2 <= 0.002 (G.R5.3, G.R5.nf) or are mediated by a confound (G.R5.sg: class_number magnitude, not crystallographic structure). No genuine cross-domain bridge emerges.

### 5.5 EC divisibility tests are clean kills

The div-by-prime tests (G.R2.d7/d11/d13) show no rank-dependent divisibility for primes > 5. Prior rounds found effects at p=2,3,5 which are likely 2-adic/3-adic conductor structure, not a universal pattern.

### 5.6 Formal corpus effects are representation artifacts

All Fungrim/FindStat results (G.R1.6, G.R2.pi, G.R2.zeta, G.R4.fung, C75, C23) reflect properties of the corpus organization (Zipf's law, editorial ordering, research attention) rather than mathematical content. None qualify as discoveries.

### 5.7 C89 (torsion -> root number) is real but known

Higher torsion order biases root number toward +1 (trivial: 46%, Z/7Z: 85%). This is consistent with the well-known observation that curves with large torsion tend to have rank 0. F27 correctly flags PARTIAL_MATCH.
