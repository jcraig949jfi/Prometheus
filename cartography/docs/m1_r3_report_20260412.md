## 5. Key Findings

### 5.1 Knot polynomial identities dominate the knot batch

Four of the nine knot tests (G.R1.3, G.R4.6, G.R4.5, C84) reduce to the identity det = |Alexander(-1)| and related polynomial evaluation identities. These have eta^2 > 0.74 because they are mathematical tautologies, not discoveries. The "high z-scores" from prior genocide rounds were measuring known identities.

### 5.2 Cross-domain signals are negligible or mediated

The three cross-domain membership tests (G.R5.3, G.R5.nf, G.R5.sg) all survive permutation testing but have eta^2 <= 0.002 (G.R5.3, G.R5.nf) or are mediated by a confound (G.R5.sg: class_number magnitude, not crystallographic structure). No genuine cross-domain bridge emerges.

### 5.3 EC divisibility tests are clean kills

The div-by-prime tests (G.R2.d7/d11/d13) show no rank-dependent divisibility for primes > 5. Prior rounds found effects at p=2,3,5 which are likely 2-adic/3-adic conductor structure, not a universal pattern.

### 5.4 Formal corpus effects are representation artifacts

All Fungrim/FindStat results (G.R1.6, G.R2.pi, G.R2.zeta, G.R4.fung, C75, C23) reflect properties of the corpus organization (Zipf's law, editorial ordering, research attention) rather than mathematical content. None qualify as discoveries.

### 5.5 C72 (Brauer-Siegel) is the standout

The one finding that passes F25 as UNIVERSAL is C72: degree explains 29% of Brauer-Siegel ratio variance, and the effect transports across strata. This is consistent with the Brauer-Siegel theorem (a known result), confirming the pipeline correctly identifies genuine mathematical structure when it exists.
