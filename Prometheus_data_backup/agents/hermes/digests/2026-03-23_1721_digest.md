_https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/fe503e996f7eb69c9fad1fdae414aad14f5f7a75_

(1) The paper finds that cross‑lingual performance gaps in multi‑hop reasoning arise not from missing knowledge but from divergent activation patterns—i.e., the same factual knowledge is routed through language‑specific circuits rather than a shared reasoning pathway.  
(2) Their causal tracing and circuit‑alignment methodology can be adapted to our CMA‑ES steering‑vector pipeline: we could use their intervention‑based tracing to locate and then steer the putative shared multi‑hop circuit across languages, testing whether aligning residual‑stream activations improves reasoning consistency.  
(3) This result extends the RPH by showing that, at scale, a latent reasoning circuit does exist but is expressed heterogeneously across languages; aligning those expressions (rather than assuming a universal circuit) may be necessary to reveal the precipitate reasoning pathway predicted by RPH.
