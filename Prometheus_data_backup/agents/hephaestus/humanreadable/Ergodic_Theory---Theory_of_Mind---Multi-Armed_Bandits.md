## Nous Analysis

**Algorithm – Ergodic‑Bandit Theory‑of‑Mind Scorer (EBToM)**  

*Data structures*  
- `candidates`: list of answer strings.  
- For each candidate `c`, a **belief vector** `b_c ∈ ℝ^K` representing the probability that `c` satisfies each of `K` parsed logical features (see §2). Initialized uniformly (`1/K`).  
- An **arm‑value estimate** `Q_c` (expected consistency) and a **visit count** `N_c`.  
- A sliding window `W` of the last `L` consistency observations per arm (used for ergodic averaging).  

*Parsing (structural feature extraction)* – deterministic regex‑based pipeline produces a feature vector `f(q, a) ∈ {0,1}^K` for a question `q` and answer `a`. Features include: presence/absence of negations, comparative adjectives (`more`, `less`), conditionals (`if … then`), causal connectors (`because`, `therefore`), numeric literals and their ordering (`>`, `<`, `=`), and temporal ordering markers (`before`, `after`).  

*Consistency reward* – for a given arm `c`, compute `r_c = 1 - HammingDistance(f(q, c), f(q, gold)) / K`, where `gold` is a deterministic “ideal” answer generated by applying constraint‑propagation rules (modus ponens, transitivity) to the parsed question features. This yields a scalar in `[0,1]` measuring logical fidelity.  

*Ergodic averaging* – after each pull, append `r_c` to `W_c`. The ergodic estimate for arm `c` is the time‑average `\bar r_c = (1/|W_c|) Σ_{w∈W_c} w`. By the ergodic theorem, as `L→∞` `\bar r_c` converges to the space‑average expectation of consistency under the true answer distribution.  

*Bandit selection* – at each scoring round, choose the arm with highest Upper Confidence Bound:  
`UCB_c = \bar r_c + α * sqrt(log(t) / N_c)`, where `t` is total pulls so far and `α` controls exploration. Pull that arm (i.e., evaluate its consistency) and update `N_c`, `W_c`, and `\bar r_c`.  

*Final score* – after a fixed budget `T` of pulls, return `\bar r_c` as the EBToM score for each candidate; higher scores indicate answers that are both logically consistent (via constraint propagation) and robust under ergodic averaging, while the bandit component ensures exploration of less‑obvious interpretations.  

**Structural features parsed**  
Negations, comparatives, conditionals, causal claims, numeric values with ordering relations, and temporal ordering markers. These are captured exactly by the binary feature vector `f`.  

**Novelty**  
The combination is novel: ergodic theory supplies a time‑average consistency estimator, theory of mind informs the belief vector over possible interpretations, and multi‑armed bandits drive efficient exploration‑exploitation over answer hypotheses. No existing scoring method jointly uses ergodic averaging of constraint‑propagated rewards with a bandit policy over parsed logical features.
