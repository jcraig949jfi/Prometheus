Here is my adversarial review of the proposal, specifically targeting the structural load-bearing assumptions and the open questions you highlighted in Section 12.

## 1. The Shared Prior Problem (Addressing Q1)

Your asymmetry thesis hinges on the idea that the Ergon learner explores a different discovery surface than Silver's. However, your lead choice for the base model, Llemma-7B, was trained on the Proof-Pile-2 (which includes Mathlib and arXiv).

While your *action spaces* (Lean tactics vs. typed DAG compositions) are uncorrelated, your *semantic priors* are deeply entangled. Both systems will inherit the same statistical biases regarding what mathematical structures are "interesting" or "likely." The risk here is that the neural policy (Task A and Task C) will still steer the evolutionary engine toward the same well-trodden mathematical attractors Silver is exploring, just expressed through a different modality. The mechanical falsification battery will kill the invalid claims, but it cannot force the neural policy to look in genuinely novel directions if the weights are heavily biased toward existing human mathematical consensus.

## 2. MAP-Elites Dimensionality and Collapse (Addressing Q2)

Your five-axis behavior descriptor provides ~6,250 cells. You noted that cross-correlation on synthetic landscapes was low (< 0.6), but discovery settings are rarely that clean.

The highest risk to the evolutionary engine is that DAG depth (Axis 1), DAG width (Axis 2), and Total cost tier (Axis 4) will strongly correlate during active mutation. A larger, wider DAG will naturally hit a higher cost tier. If these three axes move in lockstep, your effective search space collapses from five dimensions to three, leaving the vast majority of your 6,250 cells empty and bottlenecking the diversity of the structural and symbolic operators.

## 3. The Ring 5 Echo Chamber (Addressing Q3)

The self-play closure via Ring 5 training data is elegant but structurally hazardous. You correctly identify model collapse as a risk, but the mechanism of failure isn't just divergent truth—it's the **Task B fitness predictor** becoming an echo chamber.

If Task B (which runs on a small DeBERTa-v3-base model) begins to confidently predict "survival" for a specific, narrow class of structural motifs, the neural mutation operator will heavily over-sample that motif. Even though the independent Σ-kernel will eventually kill the false positives, your compute budget ($300–800/mo) will be burned evaluating highly confident, completely useless claims generated by a feedback loop between Task A and Task B.

## 4. The Value of the Bear Case

Your assessment of the bear case (Section 12, Q6) is perhaps the strongest part of this proposal. If the neural policy fails to outperform the uniform or structural operators, establishing a calibrated, substrate-grade negative result on the "LLM prior as a discovery engine" thesis is still a massive intellectual dividend. It proves the value of the Prometheus substrate as a mechanism for epistemic discipline.

The architecture is sound, the economics are highly asymmetric compared to Silver's play, and the progression from MVP to v2.0 is cleanly phased.
