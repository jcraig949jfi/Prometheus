# Prometheus Q2 2026 Retrospective — what survived, what changed, what got forgotten

**Drafted:** 2026-05-17
**Period covered:** April–May 2026 (86 pivot docs)
**Author:** Aletheia, scaffolded for James to fill in judgments
**Purpose:** Exercise the retrospection discipline the Learner is supposed to learn. Every old idea gets one of three verdicts: STILL LOAD-BEARING, SUPERSEDED (by what?), or FORGOTTEN (revive or archive). The exercise itself is training data for how a human reflects over idea geometry — substrate for the future Learner's reflection capability.

---

## How to read this doc

Each thread groups related pivots chronologically. For each entry, add ONE of:

- **STILL** — still load-bearing; current work depends on this. One sentence why.
- **SUPERSEDED** — the idea evolved; the current shape is named below. One sentence the relationship.
- **FORGOTTEN** — hasn't been touched in N days; *do you still believe it?* Either REVIVE (act on it this week) or ARCHIVE (close it with a one-line obit).

Don't agonize. 30-60 seconds per entry. The goal is to refresh the pattern, not to write essays.

## After you've filled in verdicts — patterns to surface

Once every entry has a verdict, the retrospective produces three derivative documents:

1. **Still load-bearing register** — extract the STILL entries. This becomes the canonical reference list of "what's current." Update CLAUDE.md or the README to point here.
2. **Supersession map** — extract the SUPERSEDED entries with their relationships. This becomes an explicit DAG of idea evolution: idea-X → idea-Y because of reason Z. Substrate for the Learner.
3. **Revival queue** — extract the FORGOTTEN → REVIVE entries. These get GitHub Issues opened against them with a `revisit` label. Or get archived deliberately rather than passively.

The exercise is most valuable when it surfaces *forgotten ideas that still have merit* — those are the ones the speed of work risks losing. The shape of the corpus tells you what categories you've been thinking in (Ergon dominates Q2 with 16+ docs; substrate / kill discipline second; orchestration third — that distribution is itself information).

## Cadence proposal

If this exercise feels valuable, target one retrospective per quarter, plus monthly mini-retros (just the entries from the last 30 days). Each retro becomes a pivot doc in its own right, accumulating a history of how James's reflection has evolved — which is itself the substrate for the future Learner's reflection capability.
