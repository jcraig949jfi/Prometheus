### Interpretation

**Phi-2 sits between Gemma and Qwen/Pythia.** Its high baseline (24/30) means less suppression to begin with — fewer traps are wrong. But the remaining wrong traps ARE reachable via multi-layer steering. This is consistent with the convergence theory: the suppression circuit exists in Phi-2 but is shallower than in smaller models.

Key observations:
1. **Single layers are insufficient** — max +2 flips from any single layer. This differs from Qwen/Pythia where single layers could flip 10+.
2. **Multi-layer combos are necessary** — L12+L20 at eps×2.0 gets 30/30. The suppression is distributed across layers.
3. **Zero breaks** — Phi-2 is remarkably robust. No correct trap was broken at any epsilon or layer combination.
4. **Bypass, not amplification** — cos_with_residual ≈ 0 confirms the steering vector is orthogonal to the model's natural residual stream direction.
