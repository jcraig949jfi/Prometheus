### M1: The Strategy Succession Finding

The single most important strategy finding of the entire experiment. Temperature anneal went from 19% share to 56% of recent cracks. Three distinct eras with zero human intervention:

| Phase | Hours | Dominant Strategy | Why It Wins |
|-------|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| Phase 1 | 0-8 | Mutation (39%) | Exploits local neighborhoods until exhausted |
| Phase 2 | 8-16 | Tensor top-K (4.0x random) | Systematic coverage of high-scoring regions |
| Phase 3 | 16-20+ | Temperature anneal (56% recent) | Cyclical resets escape exhausted regions entirely |

This is a standalone finding: **exploitation → systematic coverage → reset-driven exploration** is the natural succession in any tournament with diverse strategies. Each strategy solves a different problem at a different timescale. Designing for one strategy is wrong. You need a portfolio that lets the succession happen.

Final M1 numbers: 266,000 chains, 13,948 cracks, 8,727 unique compositions, tensor advantage 4.0x random (durable over entire run). Ceiling at 0.659 (scoring limitation, confirmed).

### M3: Building Blocks Are Scoring Artifacts

The finding that stings. BB chains score **worse** than non-BB chains on 4 of 5 raw dimensions:

| Dimension | BB chains | Non-BB chains | Winner |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Novelty | 0.870 | 0.985 | Non-BB |
| Structure | 0.500 | 0.548 | Non-BB |
| Diversity | 0.257 | 0.275 | Non-BB |
| Compression | 0.000 | 0.216 | Non-BB |
| Dead-end penalty | 0.000 | 0.011 | BB (less penalty) |

**Without the +0.10 bb_bonus, BB chains average 0.46 vs non-BB at 0.52.** The topology→ising pair isn't producing better compositions. It's producing *reliable* compositions that execute consistently and get boosted by an artificial bonus.

The 13 → 17,544 amplification story from M3 is partly an artifact of the scoring incentive. The ising block hit 97.2% of all BB cracks. Only 593 unique BB chains across 41,425 BB cracks — a 69.9x reuse ratio. Monoculture driven by bonus, not by intrinsic quality.

**Implication for round 2:** Remove the bb_bonus entirely. Test whether building blocks win on raw merit.

### M2: Scoring Changes the Shape of Succession

Mutation staged a late comeback on M2 (51.8% of recent cracks, up from 16% trough). This is the mirror of M1's temperature anneal surge: as novelty decays, mutation's refinement advantage reasserts itself.

**Key insight:** The scoring function doesn't just change which strategy wins — it changes *when* each strategy wins and the shape of the succession curve. On M1 (execution-heavy), mutation peaks early and declines. On M2 (compression-heavy), mutation dips then climbs back. Same tournament mechanics, different succession dynamics. Deeper than "better scoring breaks the ceiling."

### M5: Spectacular but Uncontrolled

152,560 cracks, 0.847 quality, 20,780 corridors, Navya-Nyaya logic composing with Clifford algebra. Staggering numbers. But M5 changed three variables simultaneously (operation library, chain extension, scoring) — can't attribute the quality jump to any single change.

This is the depth bonus creating a hard incentive to max out chain length, not evidence that length 5 is inherently optimal. Another scoring artifact.

## Round 2 Design

### Principle: Round 1 was discovery. Round 2 is exploitation.

Three machines. Two controlled experiments. One integration run. One shared baseline. Two controlled variables.

### Common Base (all three machines)

**Scoring:** M2's compression-primary weights, with two critical fixes:
- Replace binary compression metric with entropy measurement: `compression = max(0, (zlib_len(input) - zlib_len(output)) / zlib_len(input))`. Continuous 0-1 signal instead of binary 0.5 cap.
- Remove bb_bonus entirely. Building blocks compete on raw merit.

**Chain extension:** From M5, but tuned. Diminishing depth bonus: `depth_bonus = 0.08 * log2(chain_length - 1)`. Gives +0.08 for length 3, +0.127 for length 4, +0.16 for length 5. Prevents the length-5 monoculture — gap between 4 and 5 is small enough that the system finds the right length per composition.

**Building blocks:** Top 10 most-reused operation pairs from M1's full 30-hour dataset. Only pairs appearing in 10+ unique high-quality chains across 3+ strategies. Properly typed signatures (not "any/any"). Empirically validated primitives.

**Strategies:** All 7 from round 1 plus chain extension as Strategy 8. Temperature anneal gets slight allocation boost (M1 finding). Mutation starts at 15% allocation (earns more if it deserves it, doesn't crowd out exploration early).

**MAP-Elites grid:** chain length (2/3/4/5) × compression level (none/low/medium/high) × cross-domain score (4 quartiles). Still 64 cells but axes reflect what round 1 proved matters.

### M2 Round 2: The Integrated Package

Everything above. All promoted building blocks. Chain extension. Entropy compression. No bb_bonus.

Target: break 0.85 on raw merit (no bonus inflation). If it fails, something is wrong with the architecture. If it succeeds, clean compositions scored by a trustworthy formula — exactly what Ignis needs.

**Primary Ignis training data machine.** Every crack goes into the training corpus. Quality scores trustworthy because no artificial bonus. Chain depth variable because diminishing bonus doesn't force monoculture.

### M4 Round 2: No-Building-Block Control

Identical to M2 except no promoted building blocks. One variable removed.

Clean test: do building blocks provide real compositional value when measured fairly? Three possible outcomes:
- M2 > M4: Building blocks are real. Validated.
- M2 ≈ M4: Building blocks are reliable executors but not quality enhancers. Still useful for training, not the compositional primitive we hoped for.
- M4 > M2: Building blocks actively constrain search by biasing mutation toward narrow composition space.

### M5 Round 2: Depth Forcing

Same base as M2 but minimum chain length of 3. No length-2 chains allowed.

The experiment round 1 couldn't run. Forces the system into the depth regime. Lets the diminishing bonus sort out whether length 3, 4, or 5 is actually optimal. If length-3 chains on M5 outscore length-2 chains on M4 with the same scoring formula, depth is real.

Also the best shot at construct-then-check patterns — you need at least 3 steps for one operation to construct and another to check.

## The Monoculture Pattern (Cross-System)

The deepest finding from round 1 isn't in any single machine. It's the pattern:

| System | What Converges | What Drives Convergence | Antidote |
|--------|---------------|------------------------|----------|
| Forge (v1-v5) | 344 tools → 19 profiles | NCD scoring rewards behavioral similarity | Expanded battery + multi-frame prompts |

Same dynamic everywhere. Optimization pressure rewards what already works and suppresses alternatives. The antidote is always: change the measurement, add entry points, remove artificial incentives. Round 2 applies this lesson systematically.

### What Cracked the Gaps

**causal_intervention:** The v1 specialist failed because its regex for "leads to" chains was too loose — it matched partial words and missed the "which leads to" continuation pattern. The v2 fix: dedicated `_WHICH_LEADS` regex for chain continuation, plus a cleaner intervention-detection pipeline that handles both "intervene to block X" and "forcibly prevent X" forms. **tom_perspective_shift:** The failed attempts (`final_gap_closer.py`) tried overly generic spatial reasoning. The fix: dedicated regex patterns for the two sentence structures the generator uses ("X sees Y on her left. Z faces X from opposite side" and "From X's seat, Y is on the left. Z sits across from X"). When facing someone from the opposite side of a table, left↔right flips. That's the entire algorithm.

### Key Finding: Depth-3 Produces 13 Structural Classes

Of 255 hubs, 28 have 3+ canonical damage operators (depth-3 candidates). Of those, 21 support at least one chain. These 21 hubs fall into **13 distinct clusters** based on their binary chain signature — which chains they support and which they don't.

This is the differentiation that depth-2 could not provide.

### The Clusters (most interesting)

**Cluster 0 — "Stochastic meta-truncation only" (5 hubs):** Myerson-Satterthwaite, No Free Lunch, Quintic Insolvability, Rational sqrt(2), Social Choice. These impossibilities share a single structural pattern: randomize, elevate, cut. They are "noise-then-hierarchy" impossibilities.

**Cluster 1 — "Adaptive localization only" (3 hubs):** Heisenberg, Bode Integral, Gibbs Phenomenon. These are "partition-restrict-focus" impossibilities — the error gets localized, not randomized. Physics and signal processing, not social choice.

**Cluster 4 — "Universal hub" (1 hub):** FORCED_SYMMETRY_BREAK supports 8 of 10 chains. It is the most structurally versatile hub in the database — the universal impossibility pattern. Only Monte Carlo inversion and Stochastic meta-truncation are missing (it lacks RANDOMIZE).

**Cluster 9 — "Rich crystallographic" (1 hub):** CRYSTALLOGRAPHIC_RESTRICTION_V2 supports 5 chains including the only instance of Inverse variational alongside the Gauge → SSB pattern. This is the richest impossibility after FORCED_SYMMETRY_BREAK.

| Chain | Support | Entropy |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Stochastic meta-truncation | 10/21 (48%) | 0.998 |

| Monte Carlo inversion | 7/21 (33%) | 0.918 |
| Redistribute then reverse | 5/21 (24%) | 0.792 |
| Gauge → SSB | 4/21 (19%) | 0.703 |

Stochastic meta-truncation is the most discriminating chain (entropy 0.998, near-perfect 50/50 split). The bottom three chains (DSP, multi-resolution, discrete averaging) only appear in FORCED_SYMMETRY_BREAK and are therefore non-discriminating singletons.

### Cross-Hub Chain Flow

The most striking depth-3 result is cross-hub flow — hub pairs that can route a 3-step chain across domain boundaries:

- **Adaptive localization:** 163 hub pairs can route PARTITION → TRUNCATE → CONCENTRATE across hubs
- **Stochastic meta-truncation:** 173 hub pairs
- **Monte Carlo inversion:** 102 hub pairs
- **Inverse variational:** 0 hub pairs (fully contained within hubs, no cross-hub routing)

This means some chains are inherently *inter-domain* (they flow across impossibilities) while others are *intra-domain* (they live inside a single impossibility). That structural distinction is invisible at any lower depth.

### Answer to the Key Question

**Does depth-3 reveal structural classes that depth-1 and depth-2 miss?**

Yes, but not in the way originally hypothesized. There are no 9/9 hubs that depth-2 fails to differentiate (no hub has all 9 canonical operators). Instead:

1. **Depth-3 creates functional taxonomy:** 13 clusters from 10 chains classify impossibilities by *what processing patterns they support*, not just what operators they have.
2. **Cross-hub flow is the real finding:** Some chains (adaptive localization, stochastic meta-truncation) create dense inter-hub networks. Others (inverse variational) are isolated. This is a new structural property entirely invisible at depth 1 or 2.
3. **FORCED_SYMMETRY_BREAK is structurally unique:** Supporting 8/10 chains, it is the only hub that participates in nearly all depth-3 patterns. It is the "universal joint" of the impossibility landscape.
