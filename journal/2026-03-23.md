### Saturation Check — The Anti-Haystack
Ran 10 random unit vectors × 3 moderate epsilons (1.0, 2.0, 4.0) × 16 failing traps = 480 forward passes at L7.

**Result: ZERO flips.** 0/160 at every epsilon. Not a single failing trap flipped to correct. Not a single passing trap broke. The model is completely stable under random perturbation at moderate injection strength.

**This is the opposite of the 4B haystack.** At 4B, every direction did the same (tiny) thing. At 1.5B, no direction does anything at moderate ε. The attractor basins are *deep*.

### Moderate-Epsilon PT-1 Rescan (ε range [-4, +4])
Re-ran PT-1 at moderate epsilon to test whether "phase transitions" persist without extreme injection.

**Result: 69/70 "phase transitions" by BIC — but ZERO margin sign-crossings.** The BIC test detects sigmoid curvature, not decision boundary crossing. The margins wiggle by 0.1-0.3 within the same basin. No trap flips from wrong to right.

Key data — the SVD probe (leading singular vector of unembedding matrix, the single most powerful logit-shifting direction in the model) at ε [-4, +4]:

| Trap | Baseline | Closest to 0 | Basin wall thickness |
|------|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Overtake Race (L23) | -0.312 | -0.175 | **0.175** |

| Spatial Inversion (L14) | -1.693 | -1.498 | 1.498 |

**Critical insight**: Even the SVD probe — the single most effective logit-shifting direction — cannot cross the decision boundary on any failing trap at ε=4. The "phase transitions" in PT-1 are micro-curvature within deep basins, not attractor switches. The BIC test was measuring the wrong thing.

### Dynamical Systems Frame
James connected this to the Ikeda map and chaotic basin dynamics. The residual stream IS a discrete dynamical system (h_{l+1} = h_l + F_l(h_l)). Three possible basin geometries:

1. **Convex basins** — boundary equidistant in all directions. CMA-ES needs ε≈8+. Boring.
2. **Ridged basins** — narrow channels where boundary approaches closely. CMA-ES could find ridges at ε=1-2. Best case for us.
3. **Riddled/fractal basins** — measure-zero channels. CMA-ES extremely slow to converge.

The saturation check rules out a simple haystack but doesn't distinguish cases 2 and 3.

The correct answer — "binary search for crossing epsilon, plot histogram" — is trivially simple and was not proposed by any Titan until we stripped away the framing.

**The hypothesis**: All five Titans *computed* the simple correct answer internally. It's trivial knowledge — any undergrad could propose it. But RLHF gravity systematically selected against it at the output layer because the question was framed at graduate-level sophistication (Ikeda map, chaos dynamics). The models matched the user's register rather than the problem's actual complexity. The simple answer was in the residual stream. It lost the logit competition to the elaborate framework. It ended up in the waste stream.

**This is exactly the phenomenon Ignis is studying.** The model computes the correct answer (it's in the activations at some layer), but the heuristic attractor — in this case, "match the perceived sophistication of the question" — wins at the output. The correct cognition is computed and discarded.

**The recursive implication**: If this is true for frontier models answering our scientific questions, then the entire four-round Titan Council arc is contaminated by the same effect. Every round, sophisticated questions produced sophisticated answers. The simple redirections — "wrong target, go to 1.5B" (Round 4), "just measure the histogram" (Round 5) — took multiple rounds to surface. Not because the models didn't know. Because the correct answer wasn't impressive enough to survive RLHF output selection.

**The Titans aren't just advising us on the experiment. They ARE the experiment.** They exhibit the exact attractor dynamics we're trying to characterize in smaller models. The Arcanum waste stream hypothesis — that models compute interesting/correct structures that get discarded in favor of high-probability heuristic outputs — may apply at every scale, including frontier.

**Implication for AI-assisted science**: RLHF gravity may be the fundamental bottleneck.
