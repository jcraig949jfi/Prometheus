# Task 6: Target Venues

## Venue Analysis

### Historia Mathematica (Elsevier)
- **Fit: EXCELLENT** -- Best primary target
- **Angle they value:** Rigorous historical scholarship connecting ancient and modern mathematics. Robson's Plimpton 322 paper published here.
- **Reviewer expertise:** History of Babylonian mathematics, history of analysis, possibly abstract algebra
- **What to emphasize:** The Babylonian evidence, transmission chain, convergent evolution. Formal algebraic framing should support the historical argument, not dominate it.
- **Risk:** Reviewers may push back on DMCI schema as anachronistic. Must handle intentionality carefully.
- **Length:** Full-length research article (20-40 pages)

### Archive for History of Exact Sciences (Springer)
- **Fit: EXCELLENT** -- Strong alternative primary target
- **Angle they value:** "The conceptual groundwork of the sciences by analyzing the historical course of mathematical and quantitative thought." Neugebauer himself published here.
- **Reviewer expertise:** Deep expertise in ancient mathematics, may want cuneiform/textual analysis
- **What to emphasize:** Mathematical rigor combined with historical scholarship. The domain restriction isomorphism would appeal here.
- **Risk:** May want more detailed textual analysis of specific tablets
- **Length:** Long-form (30-50 pages typical)

### American Mathematical Monthly (MAA, Taylor & Francis)
- **Fit: VERY GOOD** -- Best for expository version
- **Angle they value:** Accessible, high-quality exposition. Propp's "Real Analysis in Reverse" (2013) is a good model.
- **Reviewer expertise:** Broad mathematical audience; clarity and accessibility paramount
- **What to emphasize:** The DMCI framework as a unifying insight. The "aha moment" of seeing Babylonian scribes and modern engineers doing the same thing. Less historical detail, more mathematical elegance.
- **Risk:** May want less historical detail and more mathematical content
- **Length:** 10-20 pages

### Notices of the AMS
- **Fit: GOOD** -- Feature article format
- **Angle they value:** High-level expository articles with broad appeal
- **What to emphasize:** The 4000-year narrative. Visual table comparisons. Convergent evolution argument.
- **Length:** 3000-5000 words (condensed)

### Foundations of Computational Mathematics (Springer)
- **Fit: POOR** unless a formal theoretical result is included
- **Angle they value:** Fundamental research at math/computation intersection
- **What would be needed:** A formal theorem (e.g., DMCI is the unique minimal schema for TDC). Without new mathematical content beyond the framing, this is a mismatch.

### BSHM Bulletin (Taylor & Francis)
- **Fit: GOOD** -- Accessible shorter piece
- **Angle they value:** Articles across all periods of mathematics history
- **Length:** 3000-6000 words
- **Risk:** Less rigorous than AHES or Historia Mathematica

---

## Recommended Strategy

### Option A: Full Research Paper (Recommended)
1. **Primary submission:** Historia Mathematica
2. **If rejected:** Archive for History of Exact Sciences
3. **Backup:** BSHM Bulletin (shorter version)

### Option B: Expository Paper
1. **Primary:** American Mathematical Monthly
2. **Alternative:** Notices of the AMS (condensed feature)

### Option C: Two Papers
1. **Historical paper** -> Historia Mathematica (Babylonian evidence, transmission chain, convergent evolution)
2. **Mathematical paper** -> American Mathematical Monthly (DMCI framework, domain restriction isomorphism, catalog of TDC instances)

**Option C is likely strongest** -- splits the contribution along natural disciplinary lines and produces two publications from one research program.
