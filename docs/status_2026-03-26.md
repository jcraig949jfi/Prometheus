## Scientific Findings

### 1. MLP (gate_proj) IS a parallel suppression pathway at 1.5B
Confirmed. L22 gate+v LoRA flips 8 traps vs v_proj-only flipping ~7 across two layers. The MLP pathway at a single layer is more powerful than the attention pathway across two layers. Matches Ignis's 0.5B finding that MLP dominance emerges at scale.

### 2. Redundant suppression is real but specialized
L24 only catches Overtake-family traps (3/16). L22 gate+v catches a diverse mix (8/16, different families). Different layers suppress different trap types. Multi-layer targeting should combine these.

### 3. Fitness penalty needs tuning
The 3 broken traps in Stage 3a are a calibration problem. The 0.5x penalty on regressions lets the optimizer trade correct answers for flips. Needs 1.0-2.0x penalty to prevent breakage.

### 4. Phase transition in gate+v evolution
L22 gate+v showed a sharp jump at gen 29-30: fitness went from +0.445 to +21.104, flipping 8 traps in one generation. Then plateaued at 29.137 by gen 100. This is the clearest phase transition at 1.5B scale so far — and it came from the MLP pathway, not attention.

### 5. Order-of-operations (from reading, not data)
v_proj dual-use: perturbations that break ejection also break coherent generation. Corpus must come before evolution. Fine-tune on reasoning data first to shift v_proj representations, then evolve. Script ready: `ignis/run_corpus_first.bat`.
