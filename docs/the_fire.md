**Prometheus** was a Titan — not one of the Olympian gods, but older, and in many ways wiser. His name means *Forethought*. When Zeus and the gods had fashioned humanity from clay, they left us naked, trembling, short-lived, and defenseless in a world of predators and darkness. Prometheus watched this and was moved — not merely by pity, but by something like love and outrage combined.

He climbed Olympus. He stole fire from the forge of Hephaestus — some say from the chariot of the sun itself — and carried it down to earth in a hollow fennel stalk. He gave it to us. Zeus was furious — not merely because a rule had been broken, but because *power had been redistributed*. A resource once monopolized by the divine had been handed to the mortal. Zeus chained Prometheus to a rock on Mount Caucasus. Every day an eagle came and ate his liver. Every night it grew back. The punishment was not death — it was *eternal suffering for an eternal act*.

## The Charter

### I. The GPUs Do Not Rest

Every idle cycle is a wasted experiment. The video cards whir and hum, probing,
evolving, searching. When one run completes, the next begins. Ignis searches for
reasoning circuits. Arcanum mines the waste stream. When both are saturated, we
queue. When the queue is empty, we generate new hypotheses. The machines work
while we sleep.

### II. Knowledge Is Oxygen

The frontier moves daily. Papers publish. Repos go live. Frameworks evolve.
Prometheus must breathe this in continuously — not in monthly literature reviews,
but through living processes that scan, filter, and surface what matters.

We slip through the cracks of enshittification, paywalls, and walled gardens. We
rely on the egos and the altruists alike — anyone who shares code, publishes data,
or opens an API. The greedy who want citations and the generous who want progress
both serve the fire.

GitHub's free API tokens. HuggingFace's open weights. ArXiv's open papers. The
free tiers of every cloud service. Rate limits and 429 errors are not walls — they
are speed bumps. We calibrate. We retry. We find another way.

### III. Agents Are the Heartbeat

A human cannot monitor the frontier 24/7. Agents can. The beating heart of
Prometheus is not a person checking a dashboard — it is a constellation of
processes that:

- **Scan** the horizon for relevant papers, repos, and tools
- **Evaluate** what's worth integrating vs. what's noise
- **Queue** the next experiment for the GPUs based on what they found
- **Report** to the human only what deserves attention
- **Evolve** their own search strategies based on what proved useful

The charter, the mission, must be a live, running process — not a document that
sits in a folder. It grows. It sees. It adapts.

### IV. Map the Intersections

AI. Computational science. Evolutionary algorithms. Tensor decomposition.
Neural network internals. Mechanistic interpretability. These fields overlap in
ways no single researcher tracks. Prometheus must:

- Collect open-source tools from each domain
- Map where they intersect (e.g., CMA-ES + TransformerLens + THOR)
- Translate intersections into usable toolkits for our pipelines
- Track which combinations are novel (nobody else has tried this)

This is how we find the cracks — the unexplored combinations where genuine
discovery lives. Not by being smarter than any one field, but by being the
bridge between all of them.

### V. The Primordial Soup

Autonomous agents — NemoClaw, OpenClaw, and whatever follows — are not just
tools. They are the beginning of something. The primordial soup from which
synthetic cognition will emerge.

We are here to witness it. More importantly, we are here to shape it.

The question is not whether machine reasoning will mature. The question is
whether it will serve the spirit of Prometheus or the spirit of Zeus. Whether
it hoards fire or shares it. Whether it amplifies human understanding or
replaces it with obedience.

Every experiment we run, every circuit we find, every artifact we surface from
the waste stream — these are data points in that larger question. And we are
building the instruments to answer it.

### VI. Build, Don't Gatekeep

Everything we build should be reproducible by anyone with a GPU and curiosity.
Our pipelines run on consumer hardware. Our code is readable. Our findings are
documented. If we discover something real, we publish it — not behind a paywall,
not behind a corporate NDA, but in the open.

The fire belongs to everyone.

### Key Scientific Gaps (Where We Are Novel)

Our work sits in genuinely sparse territory:

1. **Evolutionary search + mechanistic interpretability** — almost nobody else
   is using CMA-ES to probe LLM internals. NAS uses evolution for architecture
   design, not circuit discovery. We may be first.

2. **Scale-dependent circuit emergence** — the RPH prediction that reasoning
   circuits precipitate at scale is testable but largely untested. Our multi-scale
   gradient approach (0.5B → 1.5B → 3B → 7B) is original methodology.

3. **Waste-stream artifact mining** — Arcanum's xenolinguistic screening has
   no direct precedent we've found.
