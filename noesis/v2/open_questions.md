# Noesis v2 — Open Questions

## 1. Textile Recognition: Is "identify which structure is instantiated" always decomposable?

**Status:** Tracking
**Source:** Athena feedback on ChatGPT EMBED analysis

The operation of recognizing which algebraic object (e.g., wallpaper group) is instantiated in a physical system (e.g., weaving pattern) has two phases:
1. REDUCE — identify invariances, collapse equivalence classes
2. MAP — classify the resulting structure within an existing taxonomy

**Question:** Is this two-phase decomposition always available? Or are there cases where the recognition step is irreducibly atomic — where you can't separate "identify the structure" from "name the structure"?

**Why it matters:** If always decomposable → basis holds. If not → potential 12th primitive around "structural recognition."

**Test cases:** Ethnomathematical systems where the recognition IS the mathematics (weavers, navigators who don't formalize — they perceive the structure directly).

## 2. Is BREAK_SYMMETRY the weakest primitive?

**Status:** Tracking
**Source:** Literature review — least categorically formalized of the 11

No standard categorical formalization of symmetry breaking as a morphism type. Bifurcation theory, Landau theory, and SSB in physics all describe it, but the categorical anchor is weaker than for the other 10.

**Question:** Is BREAK_SYMMETRY really primitive, or is it REDUCE + some constraint?

## 3. Is the basis minimal?

**Status:** Open
**Source:** Literature review caution

We've shown the basis is sufficient (100% decomposition). We haven't shown it's minimal (can any primitive be derived from the other 10?). Candidate for derivability: LINEARIZE might be LIMIT + MAP (take limit of difference quotient = linearize).
