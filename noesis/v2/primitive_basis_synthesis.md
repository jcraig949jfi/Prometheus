## Analysis: Is 11 Minimal? Or Do We Need More?

### Can FIXED_POINT be derived?
- FIXED_POINT = LIMIT ∘ MAP (iterate MAP until convergence = LIMIT)
- Banach: x_{n+1} = f(x_n) → x* (LIMIT of iterated MAP)
- Y-combinator: Y = λf.(λx.f(x x))(λx.f(x x)) — this IS composition + self-reference
- **Verdict:** Derivable from COMPOSE + MAP + LIMIT for contractive cases. NOT derivable for Brouwer (existence without construction).
- **Decision:** Keep as derived, not primitive. Note: Brouwer is a pure existence result, not constructive.

### Can ADJUNCTION be derived?
- Adjunction is a RELATIONSHIP between two functors, not a transformation of objects
- It's meta-structural: it describes when two transformations are "optimal inverses"
- Free ⊣ Forgetful: EXTEND ⊣ REDUCE (free group extends; forgetful functor reduces)
- **Verdict:** Adjunction may be a PROPERTY of primitive pairs rather than a primitive itself.
- (EXTEND, REDUCE) form an adjoint pair. (SYMMETRIZE, BREAK_SYMMETRY) may too.
- **Decision:** Not a primitive transformation, but a structural relationship between primitives. Critical for the tensor encoding but not the basis.

### Can EXACTNESS be derived?
- Exactness = "information is neither created nor destroyed at this step"
- im(f) = ker(g) means: everything that comes in from f is exactly what g kills
- This is CONSERVATION — a constraint, not a transformation
- **Verdict:** Exactness is a PROPERTY of chains, not a primitive transformation.
- **Decision:** Not a primitive, but essential as a constraint/invariant in the encoding.

### Can LOCALIZE be derived?
- LOCALIZE = RESTRICT (domain restriction) = a specific form of REDUCE
- **Verdict:** Derivable from REDUCE (projection to a substructure)
- **Decision:** Keep as derived. ChatGPT already had this right.

### Can FIBRATION be derived?
- Fibration = EXTEND + structure (parametrize extension by a base space)
- A fiber bundle E → B is: for each point b ∈ B, a copy of the fiber F
- This is EXTEND (add fiber dimensions) + MAP (structure group preserves fiber)
- **Verdict:** Derivable from EXTEND + MAP with additional structure.
- **Decision:** Keep as derived.

### Can RECURSION be derived?
- Recursion = COMPOSE applied to itself (self-referential composition)
- Y-combinator = FIXED_POINT of function composition
- **Verdict:** Derivable from COMPOSE + FIXED_POINT = COMPOSE + (COMPOSE + MAP + LIMIT)
- **Decision:** Keep as derived.

## Proposed Final Basis: 11 Primitives

```
T* = {
  COMPOSE,          # associative chaining of transformations
  MAP,              # structure-preserving homomorphism
  EXTEND,           # add structure/dimensions (with choice)
  REDUCE,           # remove structure (quotient, projection, coarse-graining)
  LIMIT,            # asymptotic collapse (ε→0, n→∞)
  DUALIZE,          # involutive correspondence (Fourier, Legendre, Pontryagin)
  LINEARIZE,        # local linear approximation (Jacobian, tangent space)
  STOCHASTICIZE,    # introduce uncertainty/randomness
  SYMMETRIZE,       # impose invariance (group averaging)
  BREAK_SYMMETRY,   # reduce symmetry group (G → H ⊂ G)
  COMPLETE,         # uniquely determined extension under structural constraint
}
```

### Adjoint Pairs (meta-structure)

The primitives come in natural pairs that form adjunctions:
- (EXTEND, REDUCE) — free ⊣ forgetful
- (SYMMETRIZE, BREAK_SYMMETRY) — impose ⊣ break symmetry
- (STOCHASTICIZE, DETERMINIZE) — randomize ⊣ average

These pairs are where the tensor encoding gets its richest signal: a transformation that EXTENDS in one lens and REDUCES in another is a bridge candidate.

## The Two-Level Architecture (confirmed by decomposition test)

The 60/60 decomposition test on `the_maths/` revealed that the primitives operate at two levels:

**Intra-domain (nodes):** MAP (58%) and REDUCE (70%) dominate. The 1,714 operations are computations within a domain — invariants, representations, measurements.

**Inter-domain (edges):** EXTEND, DUALIZE, BREAK_SYMMETRY, COMPLETE, LINEARIZE. The derivation chains describe transformations between domains — how you get from Newton to quantum mechanics, from thermo to information theory.

Same vocabulary, two organizational levels. The operations are nodes. The primitives describe the edges. The tensor is a typed graph where:
- Node types: what the operation computes (MAP/REDUCE variants)
- Edge types: what structural transformation connects two operations across domains

**Search algorithm shape:** Finding a bridge between domain A and domain B = finding a chain of typed edges (e.g., EXTEND → MAP → DUALIZE) connecting operations in A to operations in B. V1 found co-occurrence shadows of these chains (3.4x random). V2 finds the chains themselves.
