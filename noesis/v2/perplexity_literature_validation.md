# Perplexity Prompt: Literature Validation of the 11-Primitive Transformation Basis

We have proposed that ALL mathematical transformations can be generated by composition of 11 structural primitives:

```
COMPOSE, MAP, EXTEND, REDUCE, LIMIT, DUALIZE,
LINEARIZE, STOCHASTICIZE, SYMMETRIZE, BREAK_SYMMETRY, COMPLETE
```

Where:
- MAP = structure-preserving homomorphism
- EXTEND = add structure/dimensions (with choice)
- REDUCE = remove structure (quotient, projection, coarse-graining)
- LIMIT = asymptotic collapse (ε→0, n→∞)
- DUALIZE = involutive correspondence (Fourier, Legendre, Pontryagin)
- LINEARIZE = local linear approximation (Taylor, Jacobian)
- STOCHASTICIZE = introduce randomness/probability
- SYMMETRIZE = impose invariance (group averaging)
- BREAK_SYMMETRY = reduce symmetry group (phase transition, bifurcation)
- COMPLETE = uniquely determined extension under structural constraint (metric completion, algebraic closure, analytic continuation)

This basis was verified computationally (256 SymPy tests, 254 pass) across 25 derivation chains spanning classical mechanics to chaos theory, and against a stratified sample of 1,714 mathematical operations (100% decompose).

We also found that these primitives operate at two levels:
- Intra-domain: MAP and REDUCE dominate (computing within a field)
- Inter-domain: EXTEND, DUALIZE, BREAK_SYMMETRY, COMPLETE, LINEARIZE dominate (connecting fields)

## Questions for Literature Search

### 1. Has anyone proposed a similar "minimal generating basis" for mathematical transformations?
Look for work in:
- Category theory (Lawvere theories, sketches, functorial semantics)
- Universal algebra (primitive operations, clone theory)
- Philosophy of science (inter-theory relations, structuralism)
- Institution theory (Goguen & Burstall — theory morphisms between logical systems)
- Knowledge representation in mathematics

### 2. Has anyone classified inter-theory relations as typed transformations?
The Sneed-Balzer-Moulines structuralist program (1970s-1990s) formalized scientific theory change. Did they produce a finite typology of theory-to-theory transformations? Is our list consistent with theirs?

Specifically look for:
- Moulines's typology of intertheoretical relations
- Balzer & Moulines, "An Architectonic for Science" (1987)
- Stegmüller on theory evolution

### 3. Does Lawvere's functorial semantics imply a finite set of primitive operations?
Lawvere showed that algebraic theories are categories with finite products. Does his framework imply that there's a minimal set of "moves" that generate all mathematical constructions? Has anyone extended this to cover transformations between theories (not just within them)?

### 4. Has anyone computationally verified transformation decompositions at this scale?
We ran 256 SymPy tests. Has anyone else done computational verification of structural mathematical claims at this scale? Look for work combining:
- Computer algebra systems (SymPy, Sage, Lean)
- Category theory or structural mathematics
- Automated verification of mathematical relationships

### 5. The COMPLETE primitive specifically
Our discovery: COMPLETE = uniquely determined extension under structural constraint. This covers metric completion, algebraic closure, analytic continuation, sheafification, universal properties. Has anyone unified these under a single primitive before? The closest we've found is:
- Universal properties in category theory (but these are a characterization, not a primitive operation)
- Kan extensions ("All concepts are Kan extensions" — Mac Lane)

Is COMPLETE = Kan extension? Or is it something else?

### 6. The "Categorical Deep Learning" paper (arXiv:2402.15332)
Gavranović et al. (2024) propose category theory as "an algebraic theory of all architectures." How does their framework relate to ours? Do they have a finite set of primitive operations? Is their approach compatible or conflicting?

### 7. Existing computational implementations
Has anyone built a searchable graph/tensor of mathematical transformations typed by categorical primitives? The closest we've found:
- AlgebraicJulia / Catlab.jl (categorical data structures in Julia)
- MMLKG (Mizar Mathematical Library Knowledge Graph)
- AutoMathKG (LLM + vector database, 2025)

Are there others?
