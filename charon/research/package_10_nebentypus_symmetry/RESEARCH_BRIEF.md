# Research Package 10: Non-Trivial Nebentypus and Symmetry Type
## Priority: MEDIUM — explains or deepens the character anomaly

---

## Research Question

For weight-2 modular forms with non-trivial nebentypus character: what is the predicted Katz-Sarnak symmetry type? Is it always unitary? Are there exceptions? What happens at finite level?

## Context

We found that non-trivial character dim-2 weight-2 forms are 3.3x more likely to have EC-like zero distributions (orthogonal type) compared to trivial character forms. This goes AGAINST the naive prediction that non-trivial character → unitary symmetry → less EC-like.

Claude identified this as the most interesting unresolved finding. Either the prediction is wrong at finite conductor, or there's a mechanism we don't understand.

## Specific Questions

1. For the family of weight-2 newforms with a fixed non-trivial character χ and varying level: what is the predicted symmetry type? Is it always U(N)? Does it depend on the order of χ?

2. Are there "intermediate" symmetry types between orthogonal and unitary that appear for specific character orders? (e.g., character order 2 might have different symmetry than order 3)

3. The functional equation for non-trivial character forms involves χ(-1). When χ(-1) = -1 vs +1, does this change the symmetry type? Does it affect the zero distribution near the central point differently?

4. At level ≤ 5000 with character order 2-6 (the range we observe), are there computed 1-level densities in the literature? Do they match the predicted symmetry type?

5. "Self-dual" L-functions (where L(s,f) = L(s,f̄)) have orthogonal or symplectic symmetry. Non-self-dual have unitary. For weight-2 forms with non-trivial character: are any of them accidentally self-dual? Could this explain the EC-proximity?

6. The "root number" for non-trivial character forms — is it always complex (consistent with unitary), or can it be real ±1 (consistent with orthogonal)? What's the distribution in LMFDB at level ≤ 5000?
