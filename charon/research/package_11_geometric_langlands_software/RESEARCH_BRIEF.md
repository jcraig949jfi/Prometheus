# Research Package 11: Geometric Langlands — Computational Tools
## Priority: MEDIUM — context for Prometheus positioning

---

## Research Question

Is anyone building computational tools (software, databases, search systems) for the geometric Langlands program, as opposed to proving theorems? We need to know whether computational infrastructure for Langlands is a recognized need or something we're inventing.

## Specific Questions

1. Beyond LMFDB, are there other databases of Langlands-related objects? Databases of Galois representations, automorphic forms beyond classical modular forms, or sheaves?

2. Has anyone built software tools for computing with the objects in the Gaitsgory-Raskin proof (eigensheaves, D-modules, derived categories)? Or is the proof purely theoretical?

3. The SageMath ecosystem — what Langlands-related computation is available? Can you compute eigensheaves, Hecke operators on higher-rank groups, or spectral decompositions?

4. MAGMA and PARI/GP — what Langlands-specific functionality exists? Computing automorphic forms for groups beyond GL(2)?

5. Is there a "computational Langlands" research community? Workshops, working groups, or research programs specifically focused on building tools rather than proving theorems?

6. The Fargues-Scholze framework — is any of it implemented computationally? Can you compute with the Fargues-Fontaine curve in practice?

7. Any overlap between the ML/LMFDB community and the geometric Langlands community? Do the people proving sheaf-theoretic theorems talk to the people running PCA on Dirichlet coefficients?

## Key Starting Points
- LMFDB infrastructure papers
- SageMath documentation for modular forms and L-functions
- Gaitsgory-Raskin proof supplementary materials
- Any "Computational Langlands" workshop proceedings
