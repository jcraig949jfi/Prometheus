# Research Package 4: What Do Higher Zeros Encode?
## Priority: HIGH — directly explains our core finding

---

## Research Question

What do the n-level densities of L-function zeros beyond the central point (positions 5-20 in the ordered sequence of positive imaginary parts) encode about arithmetic families?

## Context

We found that clustering elliptic curves by zeros 5-19 (excluding the central vanishing region) produces BETTER rank-correlated clusters (ARI=0.5548) than using all 20 zeros (ARI=0.5456). The rank signal lives in the spectral tail, not the central point.

## Specific Questions

1. The Katz-Sarnak density conjecture relates the 1-level density of low-lying zeros to the symmetry type of the family. Does the 1-level density evaluated at higher zeros (away from the central point) still carry family-distinguishing information? Or does the symmetry-type signal concentrate near the origin?

2. The n-level density (n ≥ 2) measures correlations between zeros. Do the 2-level or 3-level densities of zeros in the range γ₅ to γ₂₀ differ between rank 0 and rank 1 families? Is there theoretical prediction for this?

3. In the explicit formula connecting zeros to primes (Weil explicit formula), the contribution of higher zeros to the sum over primes is weighted differently than the first zero. Does this weighting predict that higher zeros encode different arithmetic information?

4. The GUE hypothesis predicts universal behavior for high zeros. At what height does "universal" kick in? Are zeros 5-20 for conductor ≤ 5000 still in the "arithmetic" regime or already in the "universal" regime?

5. Are there papers that specifically study the information content of individual zeros γₙ as a function of n? Something like "the nth zero carries X bits of information about invariant Y"?
