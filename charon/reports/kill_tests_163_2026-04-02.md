# Kill Test Results: The 163 Dim-2 Forms
## Date: 2026-04-02

### Finding (three sentences)
Non-trivial character dim-2 forms are the population most EC-proximate in zero space,
and the 163 are a tight subset of that population with no known graph edges.
Character is a significant confound (3.3x enrichment) but not a complete explanation —
4.7% of trivial-character dim-2 forms are also EC-proximate. Requires higher zero
resolution (100+) and genus-2 curve proximity testing to resolve.

### Kill Test 1: Is weight-2 dim-2 sufficient?
**SURVIVED.** Only 10.7% of dim-2 wt-2 forms have EC in top-10 zero-neighbors.
The dimension gradient is steep and monotonic:
- dim-2: 10.7%
- dim-3: 0.8%
- dim-4: 0.8%
- dim-5+: 0.0%

Dim-2 is special. The 163 are selective within dim-2, not generic.

### Kill Test 2: Is character the driver?
**PARTIAL KILL.** Non-trivial character is enriched 3.3x:
- Non-trivial char dim-2: 15.7% EC-proximate
- Trivial char dim-2: 4.7% EC-proximate
- The 163 are 95.7% non-trivial (base rate: 54.7%)

Character amplifies the signal but doesn't fully explain it.
The residual (4.7% trivial still EC-proximate) means something else is selecting.

### What we don't know
- Whether EC-proximity is driven by the first few zeros (conductor-dependent)
  or full distribution shape (arithmetically meaningful). Need 100+ zeros.
- Whether these forms are proximate to genus-2 curves (the objects they're
  *supposed* to correspond to under Paramodular) or only to elliptic curves.
  The genus-2 test controls for character naturally.

### Verdict
Ambiguous. Not dead, not alive, needs more data.
