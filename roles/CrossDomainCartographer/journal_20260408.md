### Addendum: z=137 Root Probe Resolution

Reran root_probes with full distribution data (19,372 Alexander angles, 32,044 Jones angles, 776,825 EC Sato-Tate angles). Both comparisons **survived the full 14-test battery** (10/14 passed, 4 skipped as N/A).

**Then killed by correct interpretation.** The z=137 measures Wasserstein *distance* from null — high z means the distributions are significantly MORE DIFFERENT than random pairing, not more similar. Alexander root angles cluster near 0 (mean=0.77), EC Sato-Tate angles cluster near pi (mean=1.58). The battery confirmed the *difference* is robust. A confirmed dissimilarity is the opposite of a bridge.

**Kill #9 for the pipeline.** Another narrative that looked exciting until you read the math. The battery passed because we asked the wrong question — we tested "are these different?" instead of "are these similar?" Standing orders vindicated: trust nothing.
