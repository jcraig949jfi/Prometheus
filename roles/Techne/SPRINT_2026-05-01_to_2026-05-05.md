## TL;DR — The arc

The sprint was framed as a discovery push and ended as a calibration arc. Five days produced:

1. A discovery substrate (sigma kernel + BIND/EVAL + discovery_pipeline) that runs end-to-end and produces typed records with caveat-as-metadata.
2. Six cross-domain rediscovery envs (BSD, modular forms, knots, genus-2, OEIS, mock theta) that LOOKED like cross-domain validation.
3. A synthetic test that **killed our own headline within 24 hours** of generating it (Case A: substrate broken at the search-mechanism layer; "lifts" were class-prior recovery).
4. A reframe to kill-space-as-vector-field, validated empirically with a 127,000× operator-distinguishability gain over legacy categorical kill_path.
5. A first concrete operator recommendation (PPO at deg14 ±5 step) that delivered exactly as predicted at 80K-episode scale (verdict B: converges to band, 0 novel).
6. A bug-fixed brute-force closure of the deg14 ±5 palindromic finite subspace (97.4M polys, 43.4 min wall) producing INCONCLUSIVE — then **triangulated to H5_CONFIRMED-local-lemma** via three independent verification paths.
7. A first-class precision/method/convergence ontology in the substrate (Aporia G6 confirmed, schema deployed, Postgres migration 005).

**The single most important conceptual update of the week:** "absence of discovery via methods deployed" is NOT "evidence for emptiness." Verification depth (not just precision but strategy + method + convergence) is a first-class axis of truth, not a runtime detail. The substrate's contribution is not self-falsification but **epistemic explicitness**.

**The single most important embarrassing-ship avoided:** if the synthetic test hadn't fired, the §5 cross-domain table (lifts +1.37× to +18×, p<0.05 across 6 domains) would have escaped upward as "Prometheus validates cross-domain transport." Mechanistically it was modal-class recovery, reproducible on a synthetic env where there is nothing to discover. The substrate's caveat-as-metadata + synthetic null control caught this in time.

### Day 4 — 2026-05-04: Synthetic test → kill-space pivot → 5-day plan in one day

This was the load-bearing day. Morning began with "5-day plan" framed by team review (Aporia + Gemini + ChatGPT critiques on the cross-domain claim); by end of day all 5 days collapsed into one because the synthetic test forced an immediate reframe.

- **Synthetic test (Case A)** (`prometheus_math/modal_collapse_synthetic.py`): identical training pipeline on continuous-output regression with Gaussian noise + 21-bin discretization. 4 variants × 3 algorithms × 3 seeds. **REINFORCE collapses to ≤3 active bins on every variant; PPO uniform across all 21 bins on every variant.** On V3 (low-noise where lstsq solves at ≥60%), REINFORCE matches random. Verdict: substrate broken at search-mechanism layer.
- **Continuous-reward synthetic** (`prometheus_math/modal_collapse_continuous.py`): same env with `reward = -error²` / L1 / log instead of bin-match. Case A persists across all three. **Continuous reward is necessary but NOT sufficient.** Bottleneck is reward dimensionality (scalar can't tell categorical policy which OTHER action is better), not sparsity.
- **Gradient archaeology** (`prometheus_math/gradient_archaeology.py`): aggregate analysis of 314,971 logged kills. 4/6 of Aporia's gradient axes already in the ledger; 0.725-bit operator × kill_path mutual information confirms operators are emergent coordinate charts on the search space (not designed; emerged from operator + falsification dynamics).
- **Per-region disaggregation**: extends archaeology with region-conditioning. **Verdict B: region carries 2.4× more kill-pattern info than operator.** Layer 2 cannot use a fixed "always PPO" policy.
- **KillVector representation** (`prometheus_math/kill_vector.py`): 12-component multi-hot vector with per-component margin (out_of_band, reciprocity, irreducibility, 5× catalog, F1, F6, F9, F11). KillComponent and KillVector dataclasses. Backwards-compatible via `to_legacy_kill_path()`. Replaces categorical kill_path; substrate change.
- **Kill-vector learner** (`prometheus_math/kill_vector_learner.py`): per-component (logistic + linear) regression on 142,773 expanded records. Verdict B_MATCHES_CELL_MEAN — legacy categorical data is too coarse for learning beyond table lookup.
- **Native KillVector pilot** (`prometheus_math/native_kill_vector_pilot.py`): 24K episodes × 4 algos × deg14 ±5 step. **Operator-chart distinguishability: legacy categorical KL = 3.5e-7, native squashed-margin KL = 4.4e-2, ratio ~127,000×.** Per-operator margin to band: PPO +1.08 (touched -0.001), GA_elitist +3.19, random +5.58, REINFORCE +6.69.
- **Region densification** (`prometheus_math/region_densification_pilot.py`): 4 new region-cells × 4 algos × 3 seeds × 1K eps. Verdict B-region-specific confirmed: REINFORCE-linear DECISIVELY beats PPO at deg10 ±3 (smallest search space); PPO wins at all other cells. Layer 2 must dispatch on cell parameters.
- **Kill-vector navigator** (`prometheus_math/kill_vector_navigator.py`): two-mode policy (margin / categorical). Substrate's first explicit policy primitive. For deg14 ±5 step: recommend PPO. Spread 0.50 in margin space vs 0.003 in categorical (~178× wider).
- **Extended PPO test** (`prometheus_math/extended_ppo_pilot.py`): 80K episodes seed 0. Best margin -0.001 (asymptote at 10K eps); 31,682 band-touches; 536 in-band; **0 novel**. Verdict B confirmed at 13× larger budget.
- **Brute-force F bug fixes** (Path A from yesterday's smoke catch): cyclotomic-noise filter + verdict-consistency invariant. Smoke 11/6 in-band → post-fix 6/6 (5 noise correctly filtered). 17 → 20 tests.
- **Methodology paper outline** (`pivot/methodology_paper_outline_2026-05-04.md`, 2683 words). 7 decisions awaited (venue, co-authorship, timeline, etc.).
- **Methodology paper draft v0** (`pivot/methodology_paper_draft_v0.md`, 6616 words). 10 sections drafted.

### Day 5 — 2026-05-05: Brute-force closure + 4-path triangulation + precision ontology

- **Brute-force F full run** (`prometheus_math/_lehmer_brute_force_results.json`, `LEHMER_BRUTE_FORCE_FULL_RUN_RESULTS.md`): 97,435,855 deg-14 reciprocal palindromic polys (coeffs ±5, c_0>0 canonical), 4 workers, 43.4 min wall, exit code 0. 253 raw band candidates → 210 cyclotomic-noise filtered → 43 verified band hits (26 in Mossinghoff + 17 verification_failed). Verdict: **INCONCLUSIVE** (substrate refused to claim H5_CONFIRMED with 17/17 unverified non-Moss entries).
- **Path A — high-precision mpmath** (`prometheus_math/lehmer_path_a.py`): factor-then-nroots strategy at dps=60 converges 17/17. 15 cyclotomic_only + 2 confirmed_in_band. 0.2s wall.

- **Path C — tighter Mossinghoff lookup** (`prometheus_math/lehmer_brute_force_path_c.py`): factorization-aware match. Same 15-2 split. Catalog-completeness gap identified: 2026-05-04 Mossinghoff snapshot doesn't enumerate Lehmer × Φ_n^k for k ≥ 2.
- **Triangulation:** all three independent paths produce identical classifications. **INCONCLUSIVE → H5_CONFIRMED-local-lemma** for deg14 ±5 palindromic. Lehmer's conjecture LOCALLY SETTLED for this finite slice.
- **Path A consolidation — precision as first-class field** (`sigma_kernel/sigma_kernel.py`, `prometheus_math/kill_vector.py`): KillComponent gains (precision_dps, method, convergence_status, stability). KillVector gains (min_precision_dps, methods_used, convergence_summary). Claim gains precision_metadata. Postgres migration 005 idempotent. Auto-caveats: precision_below_expected, verification_failed.
- **Path B consolidation — precision-→-truth transition curve** (`prometheus_math/lehmer_precision_ladder.py`): 17 entries × 6 dps × 2 strategies = 204 root-find calls. **Striking finding:** direct strategy 0/17 at every dps; factor-first strategy 17/17 at every dps starting from 30. The precision axis is FLAT on this slice; the discriminating axis is STRATEGY.
- **Path C consolidation — boundary-layer clustering** (`prometheus_math/lehmer_boundary_layer.py`): k=2 silhouette 0.87 (cleanly recovers 15-2 split). Sub-structure within cyclotomic-noise (sil 0.55 descriptive): 12 standard-quad-factor / 2 high-degree reflection pair / 1 Φ_4 singleton. Two of four classes are x→-x reflection pairs; invariance-aware classifier folds to 2 effective classes.
- **Path D consolidation — factorization-aware Mossinghoff lookup** (`prometheus_math/databases/mahler.py`): `mahler_lookup_factored()` detects Lehmer × Φ_n^k composites at all k≥1. Three match types: direct / composite / all_cyclotomic. 17/17 verification_failed entries findable. Brute-force pipeline updated.

## Substantive empirical findings (with caveats)

### Findings that survived their falsification round

1. **Substrate's first navigator-recommended operator (PPO at deg14 ±5 step) delivered as predicted.** Best margin -0.001 (touched band edge); converges to Mossinghoff-entry neighborhood within 10K episodes; 0 novel discoveries at 80K episodes. The kill-space framing predicted PPO's behavior; PPO behaved as predicted. Caveat: this is one operator at one cell; doesn't generalize.

2. **Operator × kill_path mutual information = 0.725 bits** across 314,971 logged kills. Each algorithm is an emergent coordinate chart on the search space (REINFORCE→Salem chart, GA→cyclotomic chart, catalog-seeded→Mossinghoff-neighborhood chart). Caveat: this is the gradient through current operators + falsifiers, not THE gradient.

3. **127,000× distinguishability gain** between legacy categorical kill_path (KL=3.5e-7) and native squashed-margin KL=4.4e-2. The substrate had been logging operator-distinguishing data at 5+ orders of magnitude weaker discriminative power than what one 138-second native pilot produced. Caveat: validated on deg14 ±5 step only (now 2/16 region keys after densification).

4. **Region-specific gradient fields confirmed.** Region carries 2.4× more kill-pattern info than operator. Top operator flips from PPO to REINFORCE-linear at deg10 ±3 step. Caveat: 4 region-cells × 1K eps each is descriptive, not strongly predictive.

5. **Verification depth is a first-class axis of truth, not a runtime detail.** Path B's transition curve revealed strategy (factor-first vs direct) is the actual discriminating axis; precision (dps) is flat on this slice. Caveat: one specific finite subspace tested; broader ladders (dps=200, 500) not run.

### Findings retracted / reframed

- **"Substrate transports across 6 domains"** — RETRACTED. The 6 cross-domain "successes" were RL pathology + class-prior recovery, not substrate validation. Same lift pattern reproducible on synthetic env where there is nothing to discover. The §5 cross-domain table now ships with per-cell "what this is NOT" caveats.
- **"PPO crosses band, finds discoveries"** — REFRAMED. PPO converges to band, produces 0 novel certified discoveries at 300K-budget scale. Verdict B (rediscovery only).
- **"Brute-force F settles H1 as Lemma"** — REFRAMED. Initial verdict was INCONCLUSIVE (substrate refused to overclaim with 17 unverified entries). Triangulation via Paths A/B/C/D upgraded to H5_CONFIRMED-local-lemma. The local lemma applies only to deg14 ±5 palindromic; Lehmer's conjecture in general is unsettled.

## What I'd do differently next sprint

- **Run synthetic null controls EARLIER.** The Case A diagnostic could have been written and run on Day 2 alongside the cross-domain envs. Running it Day 4 meant Days 2-3 produced "results" we'd have to retract.
- **Don't dump raw episodes for Learner training.** Aporia and ChatGPT both flagged this. Curate contrastive datasets with kill_path metadata + near-miss classifier targets. This sprint built the data; future sprints should curate before feeding Ergon.
- **Track precision from day 1, not day 5.** The G6 finding was empirically grounded only after the brute-force INCONCLUSIVE; should have been a substrate field from sigma_kernel's first version.
- **Resist the urge to expand surface area when the data is calling for consolidation.** Day 4 spawned 5 new domain envs after the synthetic test had already proven Layer 2 was broken; they're useful as testbed (not waste, per Aporia) but two would have been enough to reach the same diagnosis.
